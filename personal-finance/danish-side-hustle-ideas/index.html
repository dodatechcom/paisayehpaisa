50 Side Hustle Ideas for Denmark: Earn Extra Money

Side hustles are a great way to earn extra money, build skills, and potentially grow into a full-time business. Here are 50 practical ideas for Denmark.

Freelancing & Consulting

  1. Business consulting: leverage professional expertise (DKK 500-2,000/hour)
  2. IT consulting: help businesses with technology (DKK 600-1,500/hour)
  3. Marketing consulting: social media, SEO, content strategy (DKK 400-1,000/hour)
  4. Financial consulting: budgeting, investment advice (DKK 500-1,500/hour)
  5. HR consulting: recruitment, training (DKK 400-1,000/hour)

Creative Services

  1. Graphic design: logos, branding, marketing materials (DKK 300-800/hour)
  2. Web design: build websites for small businesses (DKK 5,000-20,000/project)
  3. Photography: events, portraits, product photography (DKK 2,000-10,000/event)
  4. Video production: promotional videos, YouTube content (DKK 5,000-30,000/project)
  5. Copywriting: blog posts, website content (DKK 500-1,500/article)

Writing & Translation

  1. Translation: Danish to English or vice versa (DKK 0.50-1.00/word)
  2. Technical writing: manuals, documentation (DKK 600-1,200/hour)
  3. Ghostwriting: books, articles (DKK 5,000-20,000/project)
  4. Editing/proofreading: Danish or English texts (DKK 300-600/hour)
  5. Content creation: blog posts, social media (DKK 300-800/article)

Education & Tutoring

  1. Language tutoring: English, Danish, other languages (DKK 200-500/hour)
  2. Math/science tutoring: primary to university level (DKK 200-400/hour)
  3. Music lessons: piano, guitar, vocals (DKK 200-400/hour)
  4. Test preparation: SAT, GMAT, language exams (DKK 300-600/hour)
  5. Online courses: create and sell on platforms (DKK 500-5,000/student)

Technology & Development

  1. App development: mobile apps for businesses (DKK 10,000-50,000/project)
  2. Software development: custom solutions (DKK 600-1,500/hour)
  3. Data analysis: help businesses understand data (DKK 500-1,200/hour)
  4. AI/automation: implement AI solutions (DKK 800-2,000/hour)
  5. IT support: help small businesses with tech issues (DKK 300-600/hour)

Delivery & Gig Economy

  1. Wolt delivery: food delivery (DKK 100-200/hour)
  2. Uber: if eligible (DKK 150-300/hour)
  3. Just Eat delivery: food delivery (DKK 80-150/hour)
  4. Hungry delivery: food delivery (DKK 80-150/hour)
  5. Glovo delivery: various deliveries (DKK 80-150/hour)

Rental & Sharing

  1. Airbnb: rent out spare room or property (DKK 500-2,000/night)
  2. Room rental: long-term room rental (DKK 3,000-8,000/month)
  3. Car sharing: rent out your car (GoMore) (DKK 200-500/day)
  4. Tool sharing: rent out tools and equipment (DKK 50-200/day)
  5. Storage space: rent out garage or storage (DKK 500-2,000/month)

Services

  1. Cleaning: office or home cleaning (DKK 150-250/hour)
  2. Dog walking/pet sitting: (DKK 100-200/walk, DKK 500-1,000/night)
  3. Handyman work: furniture assembly, repairs (DKK 200-400/hour)
  4. Gardening: lawn care, landscaping (DKK 200-400/hour)
  5. Personal shopping: help people shop (DKK 200-500/hour)

Sales & E-commerce

  1. eBay/DBA: sell second-hand items (variable)
  2. Etsy: sell handmade or vintage items (variable)
  3. Vinted: sell clothes (variable)
  4. Amazon FBA: sell products via Amazon (variable)
  5. Dropshipping: sell without holding inventory (variable)

Content Creation

  1. YouTube: create video content (ads, sponsors)
  2. Blogging: write about your expertise (ads, affiliates)
  3. Podcasting: create audio content (sponsors, ads)
  4. Social media influencing: build audience, partner with brands (variable)
  5. Stock photography: sell photos online (DKK 50-500/photo)

Tax on Side Hustles in Denmark

When to Register

  • Below DKK 50,000/year: can report as hobby income
  • Above DKK 50,000/year: must register as self-employed (virksomhed) via Virk.dk

Tax Rates

  • AM-bidrag: 8% of profit
  • Income tax: marginal rate (up to 52.5% including AM-bidrag)
  • Total: typically 40-52% depending on total income

Deductible Expenses

  • Home office: DKK 28,000/year standard deduction
  • Equipment: full deduction if < DKK 14,300
  • Software/subscriptions: fully deductible
  • Phone/internet: business portion
  • Travel: business travel costs
  • Education: courses relevant to your business

Example

Side hustle income: DKK 100,000/year Deductible expenses: DKK 25,000 Taxable profit: DKK 75,000 AM-bidrag (8%): DKK 6,000 Income tax (40%): DKK 30,000 Net income: DKK 39,000

Tips for Success

  1. Start with skills you already have
  2. Set realistic hours (5-10 hours/week)
  3. Register if income > DKK 50k
  4. Keep records from day 1
  5. Separate business bank account
  6. Use accounting software (Dinero is free)
  7. Don’t quit your day job until side hustle is stable
  8. Focus on one idea before diversifying
  9. Price your services based on value, not time
  10. Network with other side hustlers

Common Mistakes

  • Not registering when required (DKK 50k threshold)
  • Not keeping receipts and records
  • Mixing personal and business finances
  • Underpricing services
  • Taking on too many clients at once
  • Not setting aside money for taxes
  • Ignoring insurance (professional liability)

References

  • SKAT.dk (self-employment rules)
  • Virk.dk (business registration)
  • Dinero.dk (free accounting software)
