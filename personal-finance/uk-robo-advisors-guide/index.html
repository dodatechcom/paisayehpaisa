What Are Robo-Advisors?

Robo-advisors are automated investment platforms that use algorithms to build and manage a diversified portfolio on your behalf. Instead of picking individual funds or stocks yourself, you answer a short risk questionnaire and the platform selects a portfolio of ETFs or index funds that matches your goals and risk tolerance.

They’re designed for people who want to invest but don’t have the time, knowledge, or desire to manage their own portfolio. The platforms handle portfolio construction, regular rebalancing, and dividend reinvestment — all for a small annual fee.

Key benefits:

  • Low fees compared to traditional financial advisors
  • No need to pick individual funds or stocks
  • Automatic rebalancing keeps your portfolio on track
  • Dividends are reinvested automatically
  • Most platforms offer ISAs, pensions, and general investment accounts

How Robo-Advisors Work

The process is straightforward and takes most people under 10 minutes:

1. Risk Questionnaire

You answer questions about your age, income, investment goals, time horizon, and how you’d react to market drops. This determines your risk level — typically on a scale from 1 (very conservative) to 10 (very aggressive).

2. Portfolio Selection

Based on your risk level, the platform selects a portfolio of low-cost ETFs or index funds. A low-risk portfolio might be 30% shares and 70% bonds, while a high-risk portfolio could be 90% shares and 10% bonds.

3. Automatic Rebalancing

Over time, your portfolio drifts from its target allocation as some investments grow faster than others. The platform automatically rebalances — selling some assets and buying others — to maintain your target mix.

4. Dividend Reinvestment

When your investments pay dividends, the platform automatically reinvests them. This compounds your returns over time without you needing to do anything.

Tax Wrappers Available

Most UK robo-advisors offer three types of tax wrapper. Choosing the right one can save you thousands in tax.

Stocks and Shares ISA

  • Invest up to £20,000 per tax year
  • All gains and income are tax-free
  • No Capital Gains Tax or Income Tax on returns
  • Withdraw money anytime without losing ISA allowance
  • Best for: Most investors — this should be your first choice

General Investment Account (GIA)

  • No investment limit
  • You pay Capital Gains Tax on profits above the annual allowance (£3,000 for 2024/25)
  • Dividends above £500 are taxed at 8.75% (basic rate) or 33.75% (higher rate)
  • Best for: When you’ve used up your ISA allowance

Pension (SIPP)

  • Tax relief on contributions (basic rate gets 20% added automatically, higher rate can claim more)
  • Money is locked away until age 57 (from 2028)
  • 25% tax-free lump sum at retirement
  • Best for: Long-term retirement savings

Fees Comparison

Understanding the total cost is crucial. You pay two types of fee:

Platform Fee

This is the robo-advisor’s charge for managing your money:

  • Nutmeg: 0.45%
  • Wealthify: 0.45%
  • Moneybox: 0.45%
  • Investengine: 0% for DIY, 0.25% for managed
  • Shepper: Free
  • Wealthsimple: 0.7% managed, 0% self-invest

Fund Fee (OCF — Ongoing Charges Figure)

This is the charge built into the underlying funds:

  • Vanguard FTSE Global All Cap Index Fund: ~0.23%
  • iShares MSCI World ETF: ~0.20%
  • L&G Global Technology Index: ~0.30%
  • Typical range: 0.1%–0.5%

Total Cost

Your total annual cost is the platform fee plus the fund fee:

Performance

Robo-advisor performance depends on your risk level and market conditions. Over the past five years, UK robo-advisors have delivered average annual returns of:

  • Low risk: 3–5% (more bonds, less volatility)
  • Medium risk: 5–7% (balanced mix)
  • High risk: 6–9% (mostly shares, more volatile)

These figures are after fees but before tax. Past performance does not guarantee future results — investment values can fall as well as rise.

Important context:

  • A Stocks and Shares ISA means all gains are tax-free
  • Over long periods (10+ years), higher-risk portfolios have historically outperformed
  • Even in bad years (2022 saw markets drop 15–20%), staying invested recovers losses over time
  • The key advantage is consistency — automatic investing removes emotional decisions

When to Use a Robo-Advisor

Robo-advisors suit certain types of investors well.

Good fit if you:

  • Are new to investing and want a simple way to start
  • Don’t have time to research and pick funds
  • Want automatic rebalancing and dividend reinvestment
  • Are comfortable with a long-term investment horizon (5+ years)
  • Want low fees compared to a traditional financial advisor

Not ideal if you:

  • Want to pick individual stocks or funds
  • Are an experienced investor who enjoys managing your own portfolio
  • Want to trade frequently (robo-advisors are buy-and-hold)
  • Have a very small amount to invest and prefer no fees at all
  • Want direct control over exactly what you own

Worked Example

Scenario: Sarah, 30, invests £200 per month into a Nutmeg Stocks and Shares ISA at risk level 7.

Assumptions:

  • Monthly contribution: £200
  • Investment period: 30 years (retires at 60)
  • Average annual return: 7% (before fees)
  • Nutmeg platform fee: 0.45%
  • Net annual return: 6.55%

Results after 30 years:

  • Total invested: £72,000 (£200 × 12 months × 30 years)
  • Estimated portfolio value: approximately £220,000–£250,000
  • Investment growth: approximately £148,000–£178,000

Why the range? Returns vary year to year. A 7% average with a 0.45% fee means your net return compounds at roughly 6.55% annually. The exact figure depends on market performance, dividend reinvestment, and rebalancing timing.

The key takeaway: Sarah’s £72,000 in contributions could grow to over £200,000 — tax-free in an ISA. That’s the power of compound growth over three decades.

Tips for Choosing a Robo-Advisor

Check FCA Regulation

Make sure the platform is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). This protects you under the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) — you’re covered up to £85,000 if the firm fails.

Compare Fees Carefully

A 0.25% difference in fees might sound small, but over 20–30 years it adds up to thousands. Always calculate the total cost (platform fee + fund fee).

Consider the Minimum Investment

If you’re starting small, check the minimum. Wealthify and Moneybox let you start with just £1, while Nutmeg requires £500.

Choose the Right Tax Wrapper

Start with a Stocks and Shares ISA if you haven’t used your £20,000 annual allowance. Only use a GIA once your ISA is full.

Read Reviews and Compare

Check Trustpilot reviews, app store ratings, and independent comparisons. Look at customer service quality, not just fees.

Try Before You Commit

Some platforms offer demo accounts or calculators that show how your portfolio would have performed historically. This helps you understand the experience before committing real money.

Getting Started

  1. Open a Stocks and Shares ISA with your chosen platform
  2. Complete the risk questionnaire honestly
  3. Set up a monthly direct debit (even £50/month is a good start)
  4. Leave it alone — don’t check daily or panic during market dips
  5. Increase contributions as your income grows

The hardest part of investing is starting. A robo-advisor removes the complexity so you can focus on the most important thing: consistently putting money in and letting compound growth do the rest.
