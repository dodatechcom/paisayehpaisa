UK State Pension Forecast: Check Your Entitlement

June 16, 2026
🏷️ state-pension 🏷️ national-insurance 🏷️ retirement 🏷️ pension-forecast 🏷️ voluntary-ni

Your UK State Pension is one of the most valuable retirement benefits you will ever receive — yet most people never check how much they are entitled to. A few minutes on the gov.uk website can reveal your forecast, flag gaps in your National Insurance record, and give you time to boost your income before retirement.

How to Check Your State Pension Forecast

The quickest way to see your forecast is online:

  1. Go to gov.uk/check-state-pension
  2. Log in with your Government Gateway account (or create one)
  3. View your forecast weekly amount, full entitlement date, and NI record

The forecast shows:

  • Weekly amount you could receive when you reach State Pension age
  • Full entitlement — the number of qualifying years needed
  • Your NI record — years paid, years with credits, and any gaps

You can also call the Future Pension Centre on 0800 731 0175 if you prefer to speak to someone.

New State Pension vs Old State Pension

Which system applies to you depends on your date of birth.

New State Pension (born on or after 6 April 1951)

  • Maximum weekly amount: £221.20 per week (2024/25)
  • Qualifying years needed: 35
  • Minimum qualifying years: 10 (you receive a proportion if you have between 10 and 35)

Basic State Pension (born before 6 April 1951)

  • Basic State Pension max: £176.45 per week (2024/25)
  • Additional Pension (Serps/S2P): extra amount based on your NI record during working years
  • Qualifying years needed: 30 for full Basic State Pension

What Counts as a Qualifying Year?

A qualifying year is any year where you meet one or more of the following:

  • Paid NI contributions through employment (Class 1) or self-employment (Class 2/4)
  • Received NI credits — automatically granted when you claim certain benefits (Child Benefit, Carer’s Credit, Jobseeker’s Allowance, Employment and Support Allowance)
  • Student NI credits — credited automatically while studying full-time under age 21
  • Caring credits — Carer’s Credit fills gaps if you spend time caring for someone

If you were employed and earned above the Lower Earnings Limit but below the Primary Threshold, you still receive a qualifying year with NI credits.

Voluntary NI Contributions: Fill the Gaps

If you have gaps in your NI record, you can pay voluntary contributions to boost your State Pension.

Class 3 Voluntary Contributions

  • Cost: £15.85 per week per missing year (2024/25 rates)
  • Maximum buy-back: Up to 6 years of missing contributions
  • Deadline: 5 April 2025 for gaps from tax years 2006/07 to 2015/16

After the deadline, you can still fill gaps from the past 6 years only.

How to Pay

Contact HMRC or use the voluntary NI contributions service at gov.uk/voluntary-national-insurance-contributions. You will receive a reference number and can pay by Direct Debit, online, or by cheque.

Is It Worth It?

At £15.85 per week (£824.20 per year), each missing year you fill adds roughly £22 per year to your State Pension for life. That means you break even in under 40 weeks — every year after that is pure gain.

The Triple Lock

The State Pension increases every April under the triple lock mechanism. It rises by whichever is highest of:

  • CPI inflation (Consumer Prices Index)
  • Average earnings growth
  • 2.5%

This ensures the State Pension keeps pace with the cost of living and never falls behind.

Deferring Your State Pension

You do not have to claim your State Pension when you reach State Pension age. You can defer and receive a higher amount later.

  • Your pension increases by 1% for every 9 weeks you defer
  • That works out at roughly 5.8% per year
  • You can choose to take the increase as a higher weekly payment or as a lump sum (which includes interest)

Deferral is worth considering if you are still working, have other income, or expect to live longer.

Worked Example: David’s Pension Boost

David is 60 and has built up 28 qualifying years. His forecast shows £175 per week when he reaches State Pension age at 67.

David checks his NI record and sees 7 missing years. He decides to pay voluntary Class 3 contributions for 5 of those years.

ItemAmount
Voluntary NI cost£15.85 × 52 weeks × 5 years = £4,121
Weekly increaseApprox £22 per week
New forecast at 67£197 per week
Annual gain£22 × 52 = £1,144 per year
Payback period£4,121 ÷ £1,144 = under 4 years

After paying back the cost in just 4 years, David receives an extra £1,144 every year for the rest of his life. If he lives to 85, that is over £20,000 in additional pension income.

Tips to Maximise Your State Pension

  • Check your forecast now — do not wait until retirement to discover gaps
  • Fill NI gaps before the deadline — the 6-year window for 2006–2016 closes on 5 April 2025
  • Consider voluntary contributions — they almost always pay for themselves quickly
  • Claim Child Benefit if you are a low-earning parent — even if you do not receive the payment, registering gets you NI credits
  • Think about deferral — if you do not need the income immediately, deferral increases your weekly amount
  • Get financial advice — a pensions specialist can model your retirement income and suggest the best strategy
