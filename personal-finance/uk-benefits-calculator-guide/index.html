UK Benefits Calculator: Find What You're Entitled To

June 16, 2026
🏷️ benefits-calculator 🏷️ universal-credit 🏷️ child-benefit 🏷️ housing-benefit 🏷️ council-tax-reduction 🏷️ pension-credit 🏷️ turn2us 🏷️ entitledto 🏷️ citizens-advice

Millions of UK households are missing out on benefits they are entitled to. Research consistently shows that billions of pounds go unclaimed every year — the average unclaimed amount is around £3,000 per household. A benefits calculator takes the guesswork out of the process by showing you exactly what you could claim, based on your specific circumstances.

Why Use a Benefits Calculator?

The UK benefits system is complex. Rules change, eligibility criteria overlap, and many people assume they earn too much or have too much savings to qualify. A benefits calculator removes the guesswork by asking you questions about your situation and then showing you what you are entitled to.

Benefits calculators are free, anonymous, and take around 10 to 15 minutes to complete. They cover both means-tested and non-means-tested benefits, so you get a complete picture of the support available to you.

Turn2Us Benefits Calculator

Turn2Us is one of the most widely used benefits calculators in the UK. It is free to use and does not require you to create an account.

What it covers:

  • Universal Credit
  • Housing Benefit
  • Council Tax Reduction
  • Child Benefit
  • Pension Credit
  • Attendance Allowance
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • Carer’s Allowance
  • Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Working Tax Credit

How it works:

  1. Enter your postcode and household details.
  2. Answer questions about your income, savings, housing costs, and family.
  3. The calculator shows a list of benefits you may be entitled to, with estimated amounts.
  4. Turn2Us also has a Grant Search tool that can help you find charitable grants if you are on a low income.

Turn2Us does not share your information with HMRC or DWP. It is purely an indicative tool.

entitledto Benefits Calculator

entitledto is another detailed, free benefits calculator. It is used by many advice agencies and local authorities.

What it covers:

  • Universal Credit
  • Housing Benefit
  • Council Tax Reduction
  • Child Benefit
  • Working Tax Credit and Child Tax Credit (legacy benefits still in payment)
  • Pension Credit
  • Attendance Allowance
  • PIP
  • Carer’s Allowance
  • New Style Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • New Style Employment and Support Allowance

How it works:

You enter your circumstances into the calculator. It produces a report showing which benefits you qualify for, estimated amounts, and whether you are currently receiving everything you are entitled to. The calculator also flags if you are under-claiming — for example, if you receive one benefit but not another that you are eligible for.

entitledto is free to use and does not store your personal data after you close the page.

GOV.UK “Check What Benefits You Can Get”

The government’s own tool is the quickest option for a basic check. Available on GOV.UK, it asks a small number of questions and tells you what benefits you might be eligible for.

Advantages:

  • Very fast — takes less than 5 minutes.
  • No account required.
  • Provides direct links to apply for each benefit on GOV.UK.

Limitations:

  • Less detailed than Turn2Us or entitledto.
  • Does not calculate amounts for most benefits.
  • Best used as a starting point before using a more detailed calculator.

Specific Benefit Calculators

Some benefits have their own dedicated calculators:

  • Child Benefit calculator (HMRC) — shows how much Child Benefit you can get based on the number and ages of your children, and whether you are affected by the High Income Child Benefit Charge.
  • Council Tax Reduction calculator (local council) — each council runs its own scheme. Check your local council’s website for their specific calculator.
  • Pension Credit calculator (DWP/GOV.UK) — estimates whether you qualify for Pension Credit and how much you could receive.
  • Universal Credit calculator (various) — several independent tools estimate your Universal Credit entitlement based on your income, rent, and family circumstances.

Benefits Covered by Calculators

Here is a summary of the main benefits that calculators typically assess:

Income-based benefits:

  • Universal Credit — the main benefit for working-age people on a low income
  • Pension Credit — top-up for pensioners on low incomes
  • Child Benefit — payment for families with children (not means-tested, but subject to a High Income Charge)

Housing and council tax support:

  • Housing Benefit — helps with rent (mostly for pensioners now; working-age claimants use Universal Credit housing element)
  • Council Tax Reduction — discount on your council tax bill

Disability and care:

  • Attendance Allowance — for pensioners who need help with personal care
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP) — for working-age people with a disability or long-term health condition
  • Carer’s Allowance — for people who spend at least 35 hours a week caring for someone

Tax credits (legacy, still in payment):

  • Working Tax Credit
  • Child Tax Credit

Jobseeker’s and employment support:

  • New Style Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • New Style Employment and Support Allowance

Additional Support Worth Checking

Beyond the main benefits, there are several schemes that can save you money. Calculators may not always flag these, so it is worth checking separately.

Cold Weather Payment

You may get £25 for each 7-day period of very cold weather (below 0 degrees C) if you receive Universal Credit, Pension Credit, or certain other benefits. This payment is automatic — you do not need to apply.

Warm Home Discount

A £150 discount on your electricity bill if you are on a low income or receive Pension Credit. Check with your energy provider to see if you qualify. Not all providers participate.

Healthy Start vouchers

Free milk, fruit, vegetables, and infant formula if you are pregnant, have a child under 4, and receive Universal Credit, income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance, or Pension Credit. Apply through the Healthy Start website.

Free School Meals

Your children automatically qualify for free school meals if you receive Universal Credit (with earnings below £7,400 a year), income support, income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance, or Pension Credit.

Worked Example: Single Parent

Let us look at how a benefits calculator works in practice.

Rina’s situation:

  • Single parent with 2 children (aged 5 and 8)
  • Working part-time, earning £1,000 per month
  • Rent: £800 per month
  • Council tax: Band B
  • Savings: £500

Rina uses the Turn2Us calculator and enters her details. Here is what it shows:

BenefitEstimated monthly amount
Universal Credit£600
Child Benefit (2 children)£184
Council Tax Reduction£100
Free School Meals2 children (saving approximately £100/month)

Total additional income: approximately £984 per month

Without the calculator, Rina might not have known she was entitled to Council Tax Reduction or that her children qualified for Free School Meals. Over a year, the total comes to nearly £12,000 in support she was not claiming.

Tips for Getting the Most from Benefits Calculators

  1. Use more than one calculator. Different calculators may give slightly different results because they ask different questions. Using Turn2Us, entitledto, and GOV.UK together gives the most complete picture.

  2. Check annually. Your circumstances change, and so do benefit rates. Run through a calculator at least once a year, or whenever something significant happens — a change in income, a new child, a move, or a change in health.

  3. Report changes to DWP. If your circumstances change after you start receiving benefits, report it promptly. Under-reporting income can lead to overpayments that you will have to repay. Over-reporting means you miss out.

  4. Keep records. Save or print your calculator results. If you later apply for benefits and are turned down, the calculator output can help you understand whether an appeal might be worthwhile.

  5. Get help from Citizens Advice. If the calculator shows you are eligible but you are not sure how to apply, or if you have been turned down and want to challenge the decision, Citizens Advice (citizensadvice.org.uk or call 0800 144 8444) provides free, confidential help with benefits claims and appeals.

  6. Check for grants. Turn2Us has a Grant Search tool that identifies charitable grants you may qualify for. Many charities offer one-off payments for furniture, white goods, or emergency costs.

  7. Don’t assume you won’t qualify. Many people on moderate incomes still qualify for at least one benefit — especially Child Benefit, Council Tax Reduction, or Healthy Start vouchers.
