Online-Only Banks: Better Rates, Better Apps

Online-only banks have lower overheads than traditional banks, which means they can pass the savings to you through better rates and lower fees.

Monzo (UK)

Monzo is the UK’s most popular digital bank with over 8 million customers. The app is genuinely excellent for budgeting.

Key features:

  • Free spending and ATM withdrawals abroad (within limits)
  • Instant notifications for every transaction
  • Built-in budgeting tools and “pots” for saving
  • Fee-free overdraft up to £500 at 39% EAR (expensive — use sparingly)
  • 18+ only

Best for: People who want to see exactly where their money goes each month.

Starling Bank (UK)

Starling consistently wins “best British bank” awards. It offers everything Monzo does but with better overdraft rates and fee-free travel.

Key features:

  • Fee-free spending and ATM withdrawals worldwide
  • Overdraft from 15% EAR (much cheaper than Monzo)
  • In-app cheque deposits
  • Joint accounts available
  • Business accounts available

Best for: Travellers and anyone wanting a full-service digital bank.

Chase (US)

Chase’s checking account is one of the best in the US for everyday spending. No monthly fee with a $1,500 minimum balance or qualifying direct deposit.

Key features:

  • 1% cashback on debit card purchases (up to $500/month)
  • Free ATM access at 16,000+ Chase ATMs
  • Early direct deposit (up to 2 days early)
  • Zelle integration for instant transfers
  • No foreign transaction fees on select accounts

Best for: People who want cashback on everyday spending without a credit card.

Discover Checking (US)

Discover offers one of the few debit cards with genuine cashback rewards.

Key features:

  • 1% cashback on debit card purchases (up to $3,000/month)
  • No fees — no monthly fee, no ATM fees (reimbursed up to $10/month)
  • Free checks
  • FDIC insured

Best for: People who prefer debit over credit and want cashback.

Ally Bank (US)

Ally is best known for its high-yield savings accounts but also offers a solid checking account.

Key features:

  • No monthly maintenance fees
  • 0.25% APY on checking balances
  • Free ATM access (reimbursed up to $10/month)
  • Early direct deposit
  • Zelle integration

Best for: Anyone who wants checking and savings under one roof with competitive rates.

Packaged Bank Accounts: Worth the Fee?

Packaged accounts charge a monthly fee but bundle in perks like travel insurance, mobile phone cover, and breakdown cover. The question is whether the perks are worth more than you’d pay separately.

First Direct (UK)

First Direct offers £175 to switch (paid within 28 days) and consistently tops customer satisfaction surveys.

Standard Account (Free):

  • £250 interest-free overdraft buffer
  • Access to a linked Regular Saver at 7.00% AER
  • 24/7 UK-based customer service
  • No branches (100% digital)

First Direct 1st Account (£17/month after 6 months):

  • Everything above, plus:
  • Worldwide travel insurance for family
  • Mobile phone insurance
  • Breakdown cover

Should you get the packaged version? If you’d spend more than £17/month on travel insurance and phone cover separately, yes. Otherwise, stick with the free account and grab the £175 switching bonus.

Barclays (UK)

Barclays offers several tiers of packaged accounts.

Barclays Bank Account (Free):

  • No monthly fee
  • Access to Barclays apps and features
  • Branch access across the UK

Barclays Premium Account (£15/month):

  • Worldwide travel insurance
  • Mobile phone insurance
  • Breakdown cover
  • Fee-free debit card spending abroad

Barclays Platinum Account (£25/month):

  • Everything in Premium, plus:
  • Enhanced travel insurance
  • Home emergency cover
  • Airport lounge access

Should you get it? The free account is fine for most people. The packaged accounts only make sense if you genuinely use the included insurance every year.

Joint Accounts: What to Know

Joint accounts are useful for couples sharing bills, but they come with important differences depending on where you live.

UK:

  • Monzo, Starling, First Direct, and Barclays all offer joint accounts
  • Both holders are equally liable for overdrafts
  • Both holders can see all transactions
  • Splitting the account is possible but requires both parties to agree

US:

  • Chase and Ally offer joint checking accounts
  • Both holders have full access to funds
  • Both holders are responsible for any fees or overdrafts
  • Account closure requires both signatures

Canada:

  • Most major banks (TD, RBC, Scotiabank) offer joint accounts
  • Similar rules to the US — both holders have equal access

The UK Current Account Switching Guarantee

The UK has a unique advantage: the Current Account Switch Service (CASS). It’s free, guaranteed, and takes just 7 working days.

How it works:

  1. Open a new account with your chosen bank
  2. Request the switch (the new bank handles everything)
  3. All your direct debits, standing orders, and incoming payments are automatically transferred
  4. Any payments made to your old account are automatically forwarded for 36 months
  5. If anything goes wrong, you’re covered by the guarantee — the bank must reimburse any charges or interest incurred

What switches:

  • Direct debits
  • Standing orders
  • Salary payments
  • Bank transfers (BACS, CHAPS, Faster Payments)

What doesn’t switch:

  • Overdrafts (you’ll need to apply separately)
  • Savings accounts (keep them separate)
  • Credit cards (keep them separate)

Tip: The switching guarantee makes it virtually risk-free to shop around for better bank accounts every year. You could earn £175+ from switching bonuses alone.

How to Choose the Right Account

Ask yourself these questions:

1. Do you travel abroad?

  • UK: Starling is best — fee-free spending and ATM withdrawals worldwide
  • US: Chase Sapphire checking or a no-foreign-transaction-fee debit card

2. Do you want cashback?

  • US: Chase (1% on debit) or Discover (1% on debit)
  • UK: Cashback accounts are rare now — focus on switching bonuses instead

3. Do you need an overdraft?

  • UK: First Direct offers £250 interest-free buffer
  • US: Overdraft protection varies — Chase offers it but charges fees

4. Do you want the best savings rate alongside?

  • US: Ally (0.50%+ APY savings linked to checking)
  • UK: First Direct Regular Saver at 7.00% AER

5. Do you want excellent customer service?

  • UK: First Direct (consistently top-rated)
  • US: Ally (24/7 phone support, highly rated)

The Bottom Line

There is no single “best” bank account — it depends on your situation.

If you’re in the UK:

  • Open a First Direct account for the £175 bonus and free £250 overdraft buffer
  • Consider Starling for a daily driver with fee-free travel
  • Use the switching guarantee to move every 12 months for bonuses

If you’re in the US:

  • Chase for everyday cashback on debit
  • Ally for the best all-in-one checking and savings combo
  • Discover if you want maximum cashback on debit spending

If you’re in Canada:

  • Look at Simplii Financial (free banking, no fees)
  • Tangerine for high-interest savings linked to checking
  • EQ Bank for consistently competitive savings rates

The key is to never pay for features you don’t use. A free account at the right bank will outperform an expensive packaged account every time.
