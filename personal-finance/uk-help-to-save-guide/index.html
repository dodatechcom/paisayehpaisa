UK Help to Save: Free Government Bonus for Low Earners

June 16, 2026
🏷️ help-to-save 🏷️ savings 🏷️ government-scheme 🏷️ low-income 🏷️ universal-credit 🏷️ working-tax-credit 🏷️ bonus 🏷️ emergency-fund

Help to Save is a UK Government savings scheme designed to help people on low incomes build a savings habit. It offers a 50% government bonus on everything you save, up to £50 per month, over a four-year period. If you are receiving certain benefits or earn below a threshold, this scheme could put up to £1,200 of free money into your pocket.

What Is Help to Save?

Help to Save is a government-backed savings account that rewards you for saving regularly. For every £1 you save, the government adds 50p. The scheme is designed to encourage people on low incomes to start saving, even if they can only afford small amounts each month.

The account runs for four years. You can save between £1 and £50 each calendar month, and the government tops up your savings with a 50% bonus. The maximum bonus you can earn is £1,200 over the full four years.

Eligibility

You can open a Help to Save account if you:

  • Are receiving Universal Credit
  • Are receiving Working Tax Credit
  • Earn less than £13,400 per year (this applies if you are not claiming the above benefits but meet the income threshold)

You must be a UK resident and aged 16 or over. The scheme is open to UK citizens and certain qualifying nationals.

If you are already claiming Universal Credit, your earnings and savings will be assessed as part of your claim. Help to Save does not affect your benefit payments — the bonus is treated as savings and does not count as income.

How the Bonus Works

The bonus is straightforward: the government adds 50p for every £1 you save. Here is the key information:

You do not need to save the maximum £50 every month. Even saving £10 or £20 per month will earn you a bonus. The government calculates the bonus based on the lowest balance in each two-year period, which is an important detail covered below.

How to Open an Account

You can open a Help to Save account online at help-to-save.citizensadvice.org.uk. The process is quick and you will need:

  • Your National Insurance number
  • Details of your benefits or income
  • A UK bank account for the savings to be deposited into

Once open, you can set up a standing order from your bank account to save regularly. You can also make one-off payments into the account, but you cannot exceed £50 in any calendar month.

Bonus Payments

The bonus is paid in two stages:

First Bonus (After 2 Years)

After your account has been open for two years, the government calculates your bonus based on the lowest balance held during those 24 months. You receive 50% of that lowest balance, up to a maximum of £600.

Second Bonus (After 4 Years)

After the full four years, the government pays a second bonus. This is calculated on the lowest balance held during months 25 to 48. Again, you receive 50% of that lowest balance, up to a maximum of £600.

The total maximum bonus over four years is £1,200.

Withdrawals and the Bonus Rule

You can withdraw money from your Help to Save account at any time. However, there is an important rule: the bonus is based on the lowest balance in each two-year period.

If you withdraw money during the first two years, your lowest balance may drop, which reduces your bonus. For example, if you save £50 per month for 12 months (£600 total) and then withdraw £300, your lowest balance in that period becomes £300 rather than £0. The bonus would be calculated on £300, not £600.

The same rule applies to the second two-year period. If you want to maximise your bonus, avoid making withdrawals during the first two years if possible.

After the four-year term ends, the account closes and any remaining balance is transferred to your nominated bank account.

Tips to Maximise Your Bonus

  1. Save the maximum £50 each month — This ensures you earn the full £1,200 bonus over four years. Even if you can only manage £20 or £30, every pound saved earns 50p.

  2. Do not withdraw in the first two years — Withdrawals reduce your lowest balance and cut your bonus. Keep the money in the account until at least the first bonus is paid.

  3. Check your eligibility regularly — If you start or stop receiving Universal Credit or Working Tax Credit, your eligibility may change. You remain eligible as long as you meet one of the criteria when you apply.

  4. Open the account even if you can only save small amounts — A £10 per month saving earns a £5 per month bonus. Over four years, that is £480 saved and £240 in bonus money — a 50% return.

  5. Use it as an emergency fund — The Help to Save account can serve as a disciplined way to build an emergency fund. Having three to six months of expenses saved protects you from unexpected costs.

  6. Set up a standing order — Automate your savings so you do not forget. A standing order on payday ensures you save consistently without thinking about it.

Important Notes

  • The Help to Save scheme is run by HMRC and the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP).
  • The bonus is tax-free and does not affect your benefit payments.
  • You can only have one Help to Save account at a time.
  • If you close your account early, you keep any bonus already paid, but you will not receive the full £1,200.
  • The scheme is open to all eligible UK residents, regardless of whether you have savings elsewhere.

Help to Save is one of the few government schemes that puts money directly into your pocket for saving. If you are eligible, it is well worth opening an account — even small regular deposits add up to a meaningful bonus over time.
