How to Avoid Exchange Withdrawal Limits and Account Freezes

Question from BitcoinTalk: “My exchange froze my account and won’t let me withdraw. What do I do?”

Short answer: Exchanges freeze accounts when their compliance systems flag suspicious activity. The most common triggers: large deposits, VPN usage, new device login, or transfers from flagged addresses. Prevent it by using consistent behavior and completing full KYC.

Why Exchanges Freeze Accounts

Exchanges are regulated financial institutions. They must follow anti-money laundering (AML) laws. Their automated systems flag:

  • Large deposits — Especially first-time deposits over $5,000
  • VPN or proxy usage — IP doesn’t match your registered country
  • New device login — Accessing from an unrecognized device
  • Rapid trading patterns — Unusual volume or frequency
  • Transfers from flagged addresses — Funds from a mixing service or known scam address
  • Multiple accounts — Creating additional accounts without disclosure
  • Name mismatches — Bank account name vs exchange account name
  • Unusual withdrawal patterns — Withdrawing to many different wallets

How to Prevent Freezes

Before Depositing

  • Complete full KYC (level 2 or 3 verification)
  • Use the same IP as your registered country (no VPN)
  • Use the same device consistently
  • Keep your account email and phone number current
  • Withdraw to a single wallet address initially

During Normal Use

  • Don’t deposit and immediately withdraw large amounts (let funds “season” for 24-48 hours)
  • Withdraw to whitelisted addresses only
  • Avoid mixing services (exchanges flag these)
  • Keep records of your crypto sources (exchange trade history)
  • Limit daily withdrawal frequency (1-2 per day max)

What to Do If Frozen

  1. Contact support immediately — Use the official support channel, not social media
  2. Provide documentation — Passport, proof of address, source of funds (bank statements, pay stubs)
  3. Answer questions honestly — Explain where the funds came from and where they’re going
  4. Be patient — Compliance reviews take 1-14 days typically
  5. Never threaten — Being aggressive slows the process
  6. Ask for a timeline — Get an estimated review time

Red Flags That Trigger Freezes

Some behaviors almost guarantee a freeze:

  • Depositing from a flagged address — Check with AMLBot before receiving
  • Using a new account for large trades — Build history first
  • Creating multiple accounts — Exchanges detect this easily
  • Receiving from a banned user — Even one flagged transaction affects your account
  • Using a residential IP in a different country — Exchanges log IP addresses

Verdict

Account freezes happen when you look suspicious to automated systems. Prevention is straightforward: complete full KYC, use consistent devices and IPs, let funds season before withdrawing, and never use mixing services. If frozen, provide documentation promptly.
