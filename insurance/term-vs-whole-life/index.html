Term Life vs Whole Life Insurance: Which Is Better in 2026?

June 16, 2026
🏷️ insurance 🏷️ life-insurance 🏷️ comparison

The life insurance industry pushes whole life insurance because agents earn 10x more commission. But for 90% of people, term life is the smarter choice.

Here’s the math that proves it.

The Quick Comparison

FactorTerm LifeWhole Life
Coverage period10, 20, or 30 yearsLifetime
Monthly cost$20-40$250-400
Cash valueNoneYes (1-2% return)
Death benefitGuaranteed if you die in termGuaranteed
ComplexitySimpleComplex
Agent commission50-70% first year50-110% first year

The Cost Difference

For a 30-year-old, non-smoker, $500K coverage:

TypeMonthly CostAnnual Cost30-Year Total
Term (30-year)$25$300$9,000
Whole Life$350$4,200$126,000
Difference$325/mo$3,900/yr$117,000

That $325/month difference invested in the S&P 500 at 10% = $750,000 after 30 years.

📌 The math is clear: Buy term life for $25/month and invest the $325/month difference. You'll have $750,000 at the end of 30 years — far more than whole life's cash value.

⚠️ Most whole life sales are wrong. If an agent says "buy whole life as an investment," they're likely motivated by commission. A $350/month whole life policy pays the agent $2,000-4,000 in commission. A $25/month term policy pays $50-100.
