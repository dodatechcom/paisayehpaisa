UK Home Insurance: Buildings, Contents & Combined

June 16, 2026
🏷️ insurance 🏷️ home-insurance 🏷️ UK 🏷️ buildings-insurance 🏷️ contents-insurance

Home insurance protects your property and belongings against damage, theft, and disasters. If you have a mortgage, your lender requires buildings insurance. Contents insurance is optional but highly recommended.

Buildings Insurance

Covers the structure of your home — walls, roof, floors, fitted kitchens, bathrooms, and permanent fixtures like boilers and built-in wardrobes.

What’s covered:

  • Fire and smoke damage
  • Flooding and storm damage
  • Subsidence and landslip
  • Vandalism and malicious damage
  • Falling trees and branches
  • Aircraft or vehicle impact

What’s NOT covered:

  • Wear and tear or gradual deterioration
  • Damage from DIY or renovations (unless you notify your insurer)
  • Faulty workmanship
  • Fence panels and gates (usually excluded or limited)
  • Damage from pets

Required by: Most mortgage lenders insist on buildings insurance before they’ll complete your mortgage.

Contents Insurance

Covers your personal belongings — furniture, electronics, clothing, jewellery, and anything you’d take with you if you moved house.

What’s covered:

  • Theft and attempted theft
  • Accidental damage (if included)
  • Fire and flood damage
  • Storm damage
  • Vandalism
  • Items lost or damaged away from home (usually up to a limit)

What’s usually limited or excluded:

  • High-value items (jewellery, watches, art) over £1,000-£2,000
  • Cash (usually limited to £200-£500)
  • Bicycles outside the home
  • Business equipment
  • Wear and tear

Combined Policies

A combined policy covers both buildings and contents in one policy. Usually cheaper than buying separate policies.

Policy TypeTypical Annual Cost
Buildings only£100-£150
Contents only£50-£100
Combined£150-£250

Tip: Combined policies are best for homeowners. If you rent, you only need contents insurance.

How Much Cover Do You Need?

Buildings Insurance: Rebuild Cost

Your buildings cover should be based on the rebuild cost — what it would cost to reconstruct your home from scratch. This is NOT the same as market value.

HomeMarket ValueRebuild Cost
3-bed semi-detached£280,000£180,000
4-bed detached£450,000£250,000
2-bed flat£180,000£120,000

The rebuild cost excludes land value, which doesn’t need insuring. Use the BCIS rebuild cost calculator or your mortgage valuation for guidance.

Contents Insurance: Replacement Cost

Insure your contents for replacement cost — what it would cost to buy everything new today. This is “new for old” cover, meaning a 5-year-old sofa is replaced with a new one.

Quick contents inventory (typical 3-bed house):

CategoryEstimated Value
Furniture£8,000-£15,000
Electronics£3,000-£8,000
Clothing£4,000-£10,000
Kitchen appliances£2,000-£5,000
Soft furnishings£1,000-£3,000
Miscellaneous£2,000-£5,000
Total£20,000-£46,000

Most people underestimate their contents by 30-50%. Walk through each room and add up what you’d need to replace.

Understanding Excess

Excess is the amount you pay towards a claim. It’s made up of two parts:

TypeDescription
Compulsory excessSet by the insurer based on your risk profile
Voluntary excessAmount you choose to pay (on top of compulsory)

Example: Compulsory excess £100 + voluntary excess £250 = £350 total excess per claim.

Increasing your voluntary excess from £100 to £500 can reduce your premium by 15-25%. But only choose an excess you can comfortably afford.

Key tip: Burst pipes are the most common claim. In winter, know where your stopcock is and keep heating on low when away.

Annual premium: Approximately £180-£220 for a combined policy.

Monthly cost: £15-£18 per month to protect your home and everything in it.

Tips for Getting the Right Cover

  1. Don’t underinsure — if you claim for £50,000 but your cover is £30,000, you only get £30,000
  2. Check rebuild cost — not market value. Use BCIS calculator or ask a surveyor
  3. Add accidental damage — costs 10-15% more but covers spills, cracks, and DIY mishaps
  4. Compare quotes annually — loyalty doesn’t pay. New customers often get better deals
  5. Protect no-claims discount — if you’ve built up several years without claims
  6. Declare home improvements — extensions, loft conversions, or new bathrooms increase rebuild cost
  7. Keep receipts and photos — essential for making a claim
  8. Check policy exclusions — know what’s not covered before you need to claim
