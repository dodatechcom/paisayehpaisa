Here are the cheapest crypto exchanges in 2026.

Hidden Costs Beyond Trading Fees

Trading fees are only part of the cost. Watch out for:

Spread

The difference between the buy and sell price. Some exchanges have wide spreads that cost more than the trading fee.

Example: Exchange A charges 0.1% fee but has 0.5% spread. Exchange B charges 0.3% fee but has 0.1% spread. Exchange B is actually cheaper.

Deposit Fees

  • Bank transfer (ACH): Usually free
  • Wire transfer: $10-$25 per deposit
  • Credit/debit card: 2.5-5%
  • PayPal: 2.5%

Avoid credit card deposits. The 2.5-5% fee eliminates any savings from low trading fees.

Fee Reduction Strategies

1. Use Limit Orders

Limit orders (maker orders) pay lower fees than market orders (taker orders). On Kraken, limit orders save 0.10% per trade.

2. Use Maker-Taker Fee Tiers

Trade enough volume to qualify for lower fee tiers. Even $50K in monthly trading volume can reduce fees by 30-50%.

3. Use Subscription Plans

Coinbase One ($29/mo) eliminates trading fees. If you trade over $6K/month, it’s worth it.

Kraken offers “Kraken Pro” (free) with lower fees — no subscription needed.

4. BNB for Binance Fees

Binance.US doesn’t support BNB fee discounts (Binance.com does). But if you use Binance.com outside the US, holding BNB reduces fees by 25%.

5. Batch Withdrawals

Instead of withdrawing after every trade, accumulate and withdraw less frequently. This reduces withdrawal fees.

🚨 Don't choose an exchange solely on fees. The cheapest exchange that gets hacked and loses your funds costs 100% of your balance. Security must come first.

Verdict

The cheapest crypto exchange for US users in 2026 is Kraken Pro. It offers low fees (0.16-0.26%), tight spreads, excellent security, and reasonable withdrawal fees.
