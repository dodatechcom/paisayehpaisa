Crypto Wallets Explained: Hot vs Cold, Custodial vs Self-Custody

June 16, 2026
🏷️ crypto-basics 💳 wallets 🔒 security 🏷️ beginner

A crypto wallet is how you store, send, and receive cryptocurrency. Choosing the right wallet is one of the most important decisions in crypto.

Here’s everything you need to know.

The Golden Rule

Not your keys, not your crypto.

If someone else holds your private keys, they control your crypto. If the exchange fails (like FTX), your money is gone.

Wallet Types

Hot Wallets (Online)

Always connected to the internet. Convenient but less secure.

WalletTypeBest For
MetaMaskBrowser extensionDeFi, NFTs
Trust WalletMobile appAll-around
PhantomBrowser extensionSolana ecosystem
Coinbase WalletMobile appBeginners

Pros:

  • Easy to use
  • Free
  • Instant access
  • Good for small amounts

Cons:

  • Connected to internet (hackable)
  • Less secure for large amounts
  • Can lose funds if device is compromised

Cold Wallets (Offline)

Physical devices that store keys offline. Most secure option.

WalletPriceBest For
Ledger Nano S$79Budget option
Ledger Nano X$149Bluetooth, more storage
Trezor One$69Open source
Trezor Model T$219Touchscreen, premium

Pros:

  • Keys never touch internet
  • Immune to online hacks
  • Best for large amounts
  • You control everything

Cons:

  • Costs $50-200
  • Less convenient
  • Can lose the device (but can recover with seed phrase)
  • Slight learning curve

Custodial vs Non-Custodial

Custodial (Exchange Holds Keys)

ExchangeRisk Level
CoinbaseLow (US regulated)
KrakenLow (US regulated)
BinanceMedium (offshore)
Smaller exchangesHigh

When to use: Small amounts, active trading

Risk: Exchange can fail, get hacked, or freeze your account

Non-Custodial (You Hold Keys)

WalletControl
MetaMaskFull control
LedgerFull control
TrezorFull control

When to use: Long-term holding, DeFi, large amounts

Benefit: Nobody can take your crypto

⚠️ FTX lesson: Customers lost $8 billion when FTX collapsed. If your crypto is on an exchange, it's not truly yours. Move large amounts to a self-custody wallet.

How to Set Up a Wallet

MetaMask (Hot Wallet)

  1. Install browser extension or mobile app
  2. Create new wallet
  3. Write down 12-word seed phrase (NEVER share this)
  4. Set password
  5. Done — you now control your crypto

Ledger (Cold Wallet)

  1. Buy from official website (never eBay)
  2. Connect to computer
  3. Follow setup instructions
  4. Write down 24-word seed phrase
  5. Transfer crypto from exchange

Security Tips

DoDon’t
Write down seed phrase on paperStore seed phrase digitally
Use strong, unique passwordReuse passwords
Enable 2FA on exchangesShare private keys
Buy hardware wallet from official siteBuy from eBay/Amazon
Test with small amounts firstSend large amounts without testing
💡 Your seed phrase is everything. If someone gets your seed phrase, they get your crypto. Write it on paper, store it in a safe place, and NEVER enter it on any website.
