Your 30s are peak earning years. You're making more than your 20s, but life is getting more expensive too — mortgage, kids, lifestyle creep.

The key: invest the raise, not spend it.

## Your 30s Financial Priorities

| Priority | Action | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| **1** | Max employer 401(k) match | Free money |
| **2** | Build 6-month emergency fund | $15-25K in savings |
| **3** | Max Roth IRA ($7,000/yr) | Tax-free growth |
| **4** | Invest 15-20% of income | Build wealth |
| **5** | Pay off high-interest debt | Credit cards, personal loans |

## The Math: Start at 30 vs 25

| Scenario | Start Age | Monthly | Value at 65 |
|----------|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Started at 25** | 25 | $500 | $2,610,000 |
| **Started at 30** | 30 | $500 | $1,610,000 |
| **Difference** | | | **$1,000,000** |

**You lost $1 million by waiting 5 years.**

<aside class="callout info">
📌 <strong>The good news:</strong> You can close the gap by investing more. $750/month starting at 30 = $2.4M at 65. That's nearly as much as starting at 25 with $500/month.
</aside>

## Common 30s Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---------|-------------|
| **Lifestyle creep** | Spending raises instead of investing |
| **Ignoring 401(k) match** | Leaving free money |
| **Being too conservative** | Still have 30+ years to retirement |
| **Not increasing contributions** | Should raise 1% per year |

<aside class="callout tip">
💡 <strong>Automate your investing.</strong> Set up automatic transfers on payday. If you never see the money, you won't spend it. Invest first, live on the rest.
</aside>
