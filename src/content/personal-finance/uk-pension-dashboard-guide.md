The average person in the UK holds around five or six jobs over their career. Each time you start a new role, you're likely enrolled into a new workplace pension. Fast forward a few decades and you've got multiple pension pots scattered across different providers — and no clear picture of what you've actually got. The UK Pension Dashboard is designed to solve exactly this problem.

## What Is the Pension Dashboard?

The Pension Dashboard is a government-backed digital service that will let you see all your pensions in one place. It's being delivered by the MoneyHelper (formerly The Pensions Advisory Service) and backed by legislation under the Pensions (Regulation) Act 2024.

Once fully operational, the Dashboard will allow you to:

- View all your pension schemes — workplace, personal, and State Pension — on a single screen
- See the current value of each pension pot
- Get projected retirement income based on current contributions
- Track contribution history and investment performance across all schemes

The service is expected to launch progressively through 2026, with pension providers required to connect to the Dashboard ecosystem by 2027.

## Why the Pension Dashboard Matters

The problem of lost and forgotten pensions is massive in the UK. The Association of British Insurers estimates that around **£31.1 billion** sits in lost or forgotten pension pots. That's money people have saved for retirement but have simply lost track of.

### Common Reasons Pensions Get Lost

- Changing jobs and not updating your pension provider's contact details
- Leaving a company pension scheme and forgetting which provider managed it
- Small pension pots from short-term jobs being overlooked
- Moving house and losing touch with old providers
- Death of a family member whose pension details were never recorded

Without a centralised view, tracking down old pensions is a manual, time-consuming process. The Pension Dashboard aims to change that by giving you a single login to see everything.

## How the Pension Dashboard Will Work

### Identity Verification

To protect your data, you'll need to verify your identity before accessing the Dashboard. This will typically involve:

- Entering personal details (name, date of birth, National Insurance number)
- Completing a secure verification process, similar to opening a bank account online
- Potentially using government-verified identity services

Once verified, you can search for your pensions across all connected providers.

### What the Dashboard Shows

For each pension you hold, the Dashboard will display:

| Information | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Pension provider | Name of the company managing your pension |
| Current value | The current pot value or defined benefit entitlement |
| Projected retirement income | Estimated annual income in retirement |
| Contribution history | How much has been paid in and when |
| Investment performance | How your pension funds have performed over time |
| Scheme type | Whether it's defined contribution, defined benefit, or a personal pension |

### State Pension

The Dashboard will also show your State Pension forecast, including:

- Your current weekly State Pension amount
- How many qualifying years of National Insurance contributions you have
- How many more years you need to get the full State Pension
- Any gaps in your National Insurance record

## Who's Involved?

Several organisations play key roles in the Pension Dashboard:

- **MoneyHelper** — the public body responsible for delivering and operating the Dashboard
- **The Pensions Regulator** — enforcing pension providers' obligations to connect to the Dashboard
- **Pension providers** — required to make data available through the Dashboard by 2026–2027
- **The Pensions Dashboard Programme** — the cross-industry body coordinating the technical infrastructure

## What You Can Do Right Now

The full Dashboard isn't live yet, but you don't have to wait to take control of your pensions. Here's what you can do today.

### Use the Pension Tracer

The **Pension Tracer** is a free service run by The Pensions Tracing Service. It helps you find old workplace or personal pensions when you know the name of the employer or the pension provider.

**How to use it:**

1. Visit the Pension Tracing Service website or call them on 0800 731 0193
2. Provide details of your old employer or pension provider
3. The service will trace the pension and give you the provider's contact details
4. Contact the provider directly to get details of your pension pot

The Pension Tracer is completely free to use. Be wary of any company that charges you a fee to trace a pension — these are usually scams.

### Check Your State Pension Forecast

You can check your State Pension forecast online at **gov.uk/check-state-pension**. This will show you:

- How much State Pension you're currently on track to receive
- Your qualifying years of National Insurance contributions
- Any gaps in your record
- Whether you can boost your State Pension by making voluntary National Insurance contributions

### Keep Records of All Employers

Create a simple record of every employer you've had, including:

- Company name and address
- Dates of employment
- Whether you were enrolled in a workplace pension
- Any pension provider details you can remember

This information will make it much easier to trace old pensions and will be invaluable when the Dashboard goes live.

### Don't Rush to Consolidate

It might be tempting to merge all your pensions into one pot, but this isn't always the best move. Before consolidating, consider:

- Exit charges on old pensions
- Protected tax-free cash entitlements you might lose
- Guaranteed annuity rates on older schemes
- Employer contributions you'd miss out on
- Whether the old pension has better investment options

Always take independent financial advice before moving pensions.

## Worked Example: Dave's Lost Pensions

Dave is 45 and has had seven jobs since leaving university. He's always contributed to workplace pensions but never paid much attention to the details.

Over the years, he's moved house twice, changed his email address once, and lost touch with three of his pension providers. He knows he has pensions but has no idea what they're worth.

**Using Pension Tracer now:**

Dave contacts the Pension Tracing Service with the names of his old employers. Within a few weeks, he locates three forgotten pensions:

| Pension | Provider | Value |
|---------|----------|-------|
| First job (age 22–25) | Aviva | £15,000 |
| Second job (age 28–30) | Scottish Widows | £8,000 |
| Third job (age 32–35) | Standard Life | £22,000 |
| **Total** | | **£45,000** |

Dave also checks his State Pension forecast and discovers he has 30 qualifying years out of the 35 needed for the full State Pension.

**Once the Dashboard launches:**

Dave will be able to log in and see all his pensions — including his current workplace pension — in one place. No more chasing providers or digging through old paperwork. Everything in a single view.

## Tips for Managing Your Pensions

1. **Use Pension Tracer now** — don't wait for the Dashboard to track down old pensions
2. **Keep records of every employer** — company name, dates, and any pension details
3. **Check your State Pension forecast** — visit gov.uk/check-state-pension regularly
4. **Don't consolidate without advice** — old pensions may have valuable benefits you'd lose
5. **Update your contact details** — make sure every pension provider has your current address and email
6. **Check for small pots** — pensions under £10,000 can sometimes be cashed out, but think carefully before doing so
7. **Wait for the Dashboard for a full overview** — it will give you the complete picture once all providers are connected

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will the Pension Dashboard cost money?

No. The Dashboard will be a free public service, funded by the government and the pensions industry.

### What if a pension provider doesn't connect?

The Pensions Regulator has powers to force providers to connect. Providers that fail to comply could face penalties.

### Can I use the Dashboard to move my pensions?

The Dashboard is primarily a viewing tool, not a transfer tool. However, it may link to services that help you compare and move pensions if you choose to.

### What about defined benefit pensions?

The Dashboard will show your defined benefit entitlements, including your accrued pension and projected income. However, you won't be able to transfer a defined benefit pension through the Dashboard — you'll still need to go through the formal transfer process with your scheme.

### Is my data safe?

The Dashboard uses bank-level security and identity verification. Your pension data will only be shown after you've verified your identity, and providers will only release data for pensions you actually hold.
