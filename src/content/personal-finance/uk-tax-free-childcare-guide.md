Childcare costs are one of the biggest financial pressures facing UK families. Tax-Free Childcare is a government scheme that can reduce your nursery bill by up to £2,000 per child each year — and many eligible parents are not claiming it.

## What Is Tax-Free Childcare?

Tax-Free Childcare is a government top-up scheme for working parents. For every £8 you pay into your childcare account, the government adds £2. The maximum top-up is **£2,000 per child per year** — or **£4,000 per year** for disabled children.

You can use Tax-Free Childcare for children aged 11 or under, or under 16 if the child is disabled. The scheme covers registered nurseries, childminders, after-school clubs, holiday clubs and some childcare providers.

## Who Is Eligible?

To qualify for Tax-Free Childcare, you must meet all of the following criteria:

- **Working hours**: you (and your partner, if you have one) must each work at least 16 hours per week at or above the national minimum or living wage
- **Income**: each parent must earn at least £152 per week (equivalent to 16 hours at the national minimum wage) and no more than £100,000 per year
- **Child age**: the child must be under 11, or under 16 if disabled
- **Immigration status**: you must have a UK bank account and National Insurance number

Both parents must be working, unless you are a single parent. If one parent is not working — for example, on parental leave or unable to work due to disability — you may still qualify in certain circumstances.

## How to Apply

Applying is straightforward and takes around 20 minutes:

1. Go to the [childcare service on GOV.UK](https://childcare-service.hmrc.gov.uk)
2. Sign in with your Government Gateway account
3. Enter your National Insurance number and income details
4. HMRC will check your eligibility and confirm by email or letter
5. You will receive a confirmation code

You need to reconfirm your details every 3 months to keep your account active. HMRC will send you a reminder before each renewal.

## How to Pay

Once approved, you open a Tax-Free Childcare account linked to your childcare provider. You pay money into the account and then pay your provider through the government portal. The government tops up your payments automatically.

For example, if you pay £800 into your account, the government adds £200, giving you £1,000 to pay your provider. You do not need to apply for the top-up each time — it happens instantly.

## How Much Can You Save?

The average cost of full-time nursery in the UK is around **£1,300 per month**. Under Tax-Free Childcare:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Monthly nursery cost | £1,300 |
| Government top-up (20%) | £260 |
| Your net monthly cost | £1,040 |
| Annual saving | £3,120 |

Over four years of nursery, this adds up to a saving of **£12,480**.

## Childcare Vouchers vs Tax-Free Childcare

Childcare vouchers were offered by employers and allowed parents to pay for childcare from pre-tax income. The scheme closed to new applicants in October 2018. Existing users can continue claiming until their child turns 15, or 16 if disabled.

| Feature | Tax-Free Childcare | Childcare Vouchers |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Maximum annual benefit | £2,000 per child | Up to £933 per parent |
| Both parents can claim | Yes | Yes, each from employer |
| Employer involvement | None | Required |
| Income limit | £100,000 | None |
| Open to new applicants | Yes | No (closed Oct 2018) |

Tax-Free Childcare is better for most families. The £2,000 cap per child is higher than the typical voucher saving, and both parents can each claim £2,000 — meaning a couple with one child could save up to £4,000 if both meet the eligibility criteria.

## Worked Example

Sarah and Tom have one child in nursery:

- Nursery cost: £1,300 per month
- Both parents work 16+ hours per week
- Both parents earn under £100,000

They apply for Tax-Free Childcare and each contribute £800 per month. The government tops up each contribution by £200, giving a total of £1,000 per payment. Their net monthly cost falls to £1,040.

**Annual saving**: £3,120

**Over 4 years of nursery**: £12,480

That saving alone could cover a family holiday each year, be invested for the child's future, or help with school costs.

## Tips for Maximising Your Savings

- **Apply early** — do not wait until your child starts nursery. You can apply before they are born
- **Both parents claim separately** — each parent gets their own £2,000 allowance, so make sure both of you apply
- **Check eligibility regularly** — if your income changes or you return to work after a break, you may newly qualify
- **Use for after-school clubs** — Tax-Free Childcare is not just for nurseries. It covers registered after-school clubs, holiday clubs and some childcare providers
- **Review annually** — your circumstances change as children grow. Check each year whether you are still eligible and whether the scheme remains the best option for you

## How Tax-Free Childcare Affects Other Benefits

Tax-Free Childcare does not affect your entitlement to free childcare hours (such as the 15 or 30 hours of funded childcare for 3- and 4-year-olds in England). You can use both together.

However, Tax-Free Childcare may affect entitlement to universal credit or tax credits. If you receive these benefits, check with HMRC or a benefits adviser before applying.

## Key Dates and Deadlines

- Applications are open year-round — there is no fixed deadline
- You must reconfirm your eligibility every 3 months
- Payments into your account can be made at any time during the tax year
- Unused funds in your Tax-Free Childcare account expire 12 months after they are added

## Summary

Tax-Free Childcare is one of the most valuable financial support schemes for UK working parents. With nursery costs averaging £1,300 per month, the ability to save £2,000 per child each year makes a meaningful difference to family finances.

If you have not yet applied, check your eligibility on GOV.UK today. The application takes around 20 minutes and could save you thousands of pounds each year.
