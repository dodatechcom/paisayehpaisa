The UK tax system is undergoing its biggest change in decades. Making Tax Digital (MTD) replaces the traditional annual Self Assessment return with digital record-keeping and quarterly updates to HMRC. If you are self-employed or a landlord, this will affect you — and the first wave starts in April 2026.

This guide covers what MTD means, who it applies to, when the deadlines hit, and how to prepare without panic.

## What Is Making Tax Digital?

Making Tax Digital is HMRC's programme to modernise the UK tax system. Instead of keeping paper records and filing one annual Self Assessment tax return, you will:

1. **Keep digital records** of your income and expenses using MTD-compatible software
2. **Submit quarterly updates** to HMRC summarising your business income and expenses
3. **Make a final declaration** once per year confirming your total income and tax owed

The goal is to reduce errors, make tax simpler, and give both taxpayers and HMRC a more accurate, real-time view of your tax position.

### What MTD Is Not

- It is not a new tax — the amount of tax you pay does not change
- It is not a new tax return — the information is the same, just submitted more often
- It does not apply to everyone — employees on PAYE are not affected (yet)

## The Rollout Timeline

MTD for Income Tax is being introduced in phases, starting with higher earners and expanding gradually.

| Phase | Start Date | Income Threshold | Who Is Affected |
|-------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Phase 1 | April 2026 | Over £50,000 | Higher-earning self-employed and landlords |
| Phase 2 | April 2027 | Over £30,000 | Mid-range earners |
| Phase 3 | April 2028 | Over £20,000 | Confirmed — lower earners included |

### Key Dates

- **April 2026:** If your gross income from self-employment or property exceeds £50,000, you must use MTD from this date.
- **April 2027:** The threshold drops to £30,000.
- **April 2028:** The threshold drops to £20,000.

Your income is assessed on a tax-year basis (6 April to 5 April). If your income in the 2025–26 tax year exceeds the threshold, you must be MTD-compliant from 6 April 2026.

## Who Is Affected?

### You Are Affected If You Are

- **Self-employed** — sole traders earning above the relevant threshold
- **Landlords** — property income above the relevant threshold
- **Both** — if you are self-employed and have rental income, you combine both for the threshold test

### You Are NOT Affected If You Are

- **Employed (PAYE)** — employees are not required to use MTD for Income Tax (though MTD for VAT already applies to VAT-registered businesses)
- **Partnerships** — not yet required for partnerships, though this is expected in future
- **Below the income threshold** — if your combined self-employment and property income is below £20,000 (even after 2028), you will not be required to use MTD

### Exemptions

You may be exempt from MTD if you have:

- **Age-related exemption:** If you are of state pension age or over, you may be exempt
- **Disability or severe illness:** If you cannot use digital tools for practical or health reasons
- **Religious grounds:** If using digital tools conflicts with your religious beliefs
- **Insolvency:** If you are in insolvency proceedings

To claim an exemption, you must contact HMRC and apply. You will not be automatically exempt.

## What Changes in Practice

### Digital Records

You must keep your business records digitally. This means using accounting software (or a spreadsheet that interfaces with MTD-compatible software) to record:

- **Income:** Every payment you receive for your work or rental income
- **Expenses:** Every business expense, categorized appropriately
- **VAT:** If you are VAT-registered, your VAT records are already digital — MTD for Income Tax adds income tax on top

Paper records are no longer acceptable as your primary record-keeping method. You can still keep paper copies as backup, but the digital record is the official one.

### Quarterly Updates

Instead of one annual Self Assessment return, you submit four quarterly updates per year. Each update covers a three-month period and summarises your income and expenses.

| Quarter | Period | Submission Deadline |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| Q1 | 6 April – 5 July | 7 August |
| Q2 | 6 July – 5 October | 7 November |
| Q3 | 6 October – 5 January | 7 February |
| Q4 | 6 January – 5 April | 7 May |

Each quarterly update is a straightforward digital submission showing:

- Total income for the quarter
- Total expenses for the quarter
- Profit or loss for the quarter

You do not need to calculate tax at this stage — the quarterly update is informational. HMRC uses it to build a picture of your year.

### Final Declaration

After the tax year ends, you make a final declaration by **31 January** following the end of the tax year. This is your opportunity to:

- Confirm your total income and expenses for the year
- Claim any allowances or reliefs not covered in the quarterly updates
- Calculate your final tax bill
- Pay any tax owed

Think of the final declaration as the old Self Assessment return — but now it is a confirmation of what HMRC already knows from your quarterly updates, rather than a standalone submission.

## MTD-Compatible Software

You must use software that is recognised by HMRC as MTD-compatible. This software connects directly to HMRC's systems and submits your quarterly updates automatically or with minimal input.

### Popular Options

| Software | Cost (approx.) | Best For | Key Features |
|----------|----------------|----------|-------------|
| **HMRC's free tool** | Free | Simple businesses | Basic quarterly submission — limited features |
| **FreeAgent** | £12–£24/month | Freelancers, sole traders | Built for self-employed. Excellent ease of use. |
| **Xero** | £15–£30/month | Small businesses, landlords | Full accounting. Bank feed integration. |
| **QuickBooks** | £12–£25/month | Freelancers, contractors | Strong invoicing. Good mobile app. |
| **Sage** | £12–£25/month | Established businesses | Comprehensive features. UK support. |

### Choosing Software

Consider these factors:

- **Ease of use:** Can you navigate it without a training course?
- **Bank feeds:** Does it connect to your bank to import transactions automatically?
- **Expense tracking:** Can you categorise expenses as you go?
- **Reporting:** Does it produce clear profit and loss statements?
- **Support:** Is UK-based support available?
- **Price:** Factor in monthly costs against your turnover

### Getting Started

Most software offers a free trial. Sign up, connect your bank account, and start recording transactions. The earlier you start, the smoother the transition will be when MTD becomes mandatory.

## Quarterly Updates: Step by Step

Here is what a typical quarterly submission looks like:

1. **Review your digital records** for the quarter
2. **Check categories** — ensure income and expenses are correctly classified
3. **Submit the quarterly update** through your MTD software
4. **Review HMRC's response** — they will confirm receipt and show an estimate of your tax position so far

### What Each Update Contains

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Total income | All business income received in the quarter |
| Total expenses | All allowable business expenses in the quarter |
| Profit | Income minus expenses |

### Adjustments

You can adjust previous quarterly updates if you made an error. The final declaration is where everything is reconciled — so small quarterly errors are corrected at year-end, not panicked over.

## Penalties

HMRC is introducing a points-based penalty system for late submissions under MTD.

### How the Points System Works

- **1 point** for each missed quarterly submission
- **3 points in 12 months** triggers a **£100 fine**
- Points expire after 24 months if you stay compliant
- Further non-compliance after a fine leads to additional penalties

### Key Penalty Rules

| Missed Submission | Consequence |
|-------------------|-------------|
| 1st missed | 1 point |
| 2nd missed | 1 point (2 total) |
| 3rd missed within 12 months | 1 point (3 total) + £100 fine |
| Each further missed submission | Additional points and potential fines |

### Late Payment Penalties

If you do not pay your tax on time, separate late payment penalties apply:

- 30 days late: 5% of tax owed
- 6 months late: Additional 5%
- 12 months late: Additional 5%

These are the same penalties that currently apply to late Self Assessment payments.

## Worked Example: Freelance Designer

Let us walk through how MTD works in practice for a freelance graphic designer.

### The Situation

- **Income:** £60,000 per year from freelance design work
- **Status:** Sole trader, above the £50,000 threshold
- **MTD start date:** April 2026
- **Software:** Xero

### What Changes

Before MTD, this designer would:

- Keep paper invoices and receipts
- Spend January in a panic filling out a Self Assessment return
- Submit one annual return and pay tax

After MTD, this designer will:

- Record all income and expenses in Xero throughout the year
- Connect Xero to their business bank account for automatic transaction imports
- Submit four quarterly updates (simple, straightforward submissions)
- Make a final declaration on 31 January 2028 for the 2026–27 tax year

### Quarterly Update Example (Q1: April–July 2026)

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Income (3 months) | £15,000 |
| Expenses (3 months) | £3,500 |
| Profit | £11,500 |

This update takes about 10 minutes to review and submit through Xero.

### Final Declaration (31 January 2028)

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Total income (12 months) | £60,000 |
| Total expenses (12 months) | £14,000 |
| Net profit | £46,000 |
| Personal allowance | £12,570 |
| Taxable income | £33,430 |
| Income tax (20% on first £37,700) | £6,686 |
| National Insurance (Class 4) | ~£3,648 |
| **Total tax bill** | **~£10,334** |

### Time Saved

Under the old system, this designer might spend 8–12 hours in January on their Self Assessment. Under MTD, the quarterly updates take about 10–15 minutes each, and the final declaration takes about 1–2 hours. Total annual time: approximately 3–4 hours instead of 8–12 hours.

## Tips for Preparing for MTD

### Start Now

1. **Choose your software early.** Do not wait until April 2026 to start looking at options. Most software offers free trials — sign up and experiment.
2. **Start digital records now.** Even if MTD is not yet mandatory for you, get into the habit of recording income and expenses digitally. The transition will be far less painful.
3. **Connect your bank account.** Most MTD software can import transactions directly from your business bank account. Set this up and reconcile regularly.
4. **Categorise transactions.** Get into the habit of categorising income and expenses as they occur, not at year-end.

### Get Help If You Need It

5. **Talk to your accountant.** If you use an accountant, discuss MTD with them now. They can recommend software and help set up your digital records.
6. **Use HMRC's resources.** HMRC offers free webinars, guides, and support for MTD. Take advantage of them.
7. **Join a pilot.** HMRC runs an MTD pilot programme that lets you test the system before it becomes mandatory. This is a low-risk way to get comfortable.

### Check for Exemptions

8. **If you are over state pension age**, check if you qualify for an exemption.
9. **If you have a disability** that makes digital record-keeping impractical, contact HMRC to apply for an exemption.
10. **If you are unsure whether MTD applies to you**, check your income against the thresholds. Remember, it is combined self-employment and property income — not just one or the other.

## Common Questions

### Do I Need to File a Self Assessment Return After MTD?

Yes — but it is different. The final declaration under MTD replaces the annual Self Assessment tax return. You still need to confirm your income and pay tax, but you are doing it through your MTD software rather than the old Self Assessment portal.

### Can I Use Spreadsheets?

Yes, but with conditions. You can use a spreadsheet to maintain your digital records, but it must be compatible with MTD submission. HMRC requires that your records are digitally linked to your quarterly submissions — meaning you cannot simply type numbers into a form from a paper spreadsheet.

### What If My Income Fluctuates?

The threshold is based on your gross income for the tax year. If your income drops below the threshold, you may no longer be required to use MTD — but you must notify HMRC. If your income rises above the threshold mid-year, you may need to comply from the start of the next tax year.

### What About VAT-Registered Businesses?

If you are already VAT-registered and using MTD for VAT, you are already familiar with digital record-keeping. MTD for Income Tax adds income tax obligations on top — you will need to track both VAT and income tax digitally.
