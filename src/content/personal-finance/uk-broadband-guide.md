UK broadband is a competitive market with dozens of providers and hundreds of deals. Understanding the different types of broadband, what speeds you need, and how to negotiate can save you hundreds of pounds per year.

## Types of UK Broadband

Different broadband technologies offer different speeds and price points.

### ADSL

ADSL runs over existing copper phone lines and offers speeds of 10 to 11 Mbps. It is widely available across the UK but is the slowest option. Suitable only for basic browsing and email.

### Fibre (FTTC)

Fibre to the Cabinet uses fibre optic cables to the street cabinet and copper for the final stretch to your home. Speeds range from 30 to 80 Mbps. This is the most common type of broadband in the UK and offers good value for money.

### Full Fibre (FTTP)

Full Fibre connects directly to your home using fibre optic cables. Speeds range from 100 to 1,000 Mbps. It is the fastest and most reliable option, ideal for households with multiple devices, streaming, and working from home.

## Average UK Broadband Costs

Prices vary by provider and connection type:

| Type | Typical Monthly Cost |
|---|---|
| ADSL | £20 to £25 |
| Standard fibre | £25 to £35 |
| Full fibre | £30 to £50 |

Most households find standard fibre offers the best balance of speed and cost.

## Best UK Broadband Providers

The UK has several major providers, each with different strengths.

- **BT** - Largest provider. Fibre from £29 per month. Broad coverage and reliable service.
- **Sky** - Strong TV bundles available. Fibre from £28 per month.
- **Virgin Media** - Cable network with speeds up to 1 Gbps. Prices from £35 per month.
- **TalkTalk** - Budget-friendly option. Packages from £25 per month.
- **Plusnet** - Good customer service and low prices. From £22 per month.
- **NOW** - Flexible contracts with no long-term commitment. From £25 per month.
- **Hyperoptic** - Full fibre specialist. Fast and reliable but limited coverage.
- **Gigaclear** - Full fibre in rural areas. Prices from £30 per month.

## Contract Length and Exit Fees

Most broadband contracts run for 12 to 24 months. Early exit fees typically range from £100 to £200 depending on how many months remain.

Before signing up, consider:

- How long you plan to stay at your current address
- Whether you might want to switch to a cheaper deal
- The total cost over the full contract term, not just the monthly price

Shorter contracts cost more per month but give you flexibility to switch.

## Checking Your Speed

Not all broadband types are available at every address. Before choosing a provider, check what speeds are available at your location.

Use the Ofcom broadband checker or the individual provider websites to run a speed test. Enter your postcode to see estimated speeds for your address. This prevents you from paying for speeds you cannot actually receive.

## TV Bundles: Worth It?

Many providers offer TV bundles with channels like BT Sport and Sky Sports. Bundles can seem like good value, but they often are not.

Consider these points:

- If you do not watch sport, you are paying for something you do not use
- Sports channels cost £25 to £30 per month on their own, which inflates bundle prices
- Streaming services like Netflix and Disney Plus are usually cheaper as standalone subscriptions
- Compare the total cost of a bundle against broadband-only plus any streaming services you want

Most households save money by taking broadband only and subscribing to the streaming services they actually watch.

## Router Quality

Most providers supply a free router with your broadband package. Router quality varies significantly.

- **BT Smart Hub** - Good range and performance
- **Virgin Media Hub** - Fast but can overheat with heavy use
- **Sky Hub** - Average performance, suitable for small homes
- **Plusnet Hub** - Decent for the price

If your router provides poor Wi-Fi coverage, consider purchasing a separate mesh Wi-Fi system or powerline adapters to extend coverage throughout your home.

## Tips for Saving on Broadband

- Compare deals annually - loyalty rarely pays in broadband
- Check what speeds are actually available at your address before choosing
- Do not pay for speeds you do not need - 50 to 100 Mbps suits most families
- Avoid TV bundles unless you genuinely watch sport
- Negotiate when your contract ends - call the retention team and ask for a better deal
- Consider cashback sites like TopCashback when signing up for a new deal
- Read the full contract terms including any price rises mid-contract
