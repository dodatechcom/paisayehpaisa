## The Hybrid Strategy

Many crypto users use a combination:

1. **Traditional bank** — Salary, main account (Chase, BofA, local bank)
2. **Crypto neobank** — Crypto purchases, debit card spending (Revolut, Wirex)
3. **Exchange** — Larger trades (Coinbase, Kraken)
4. **Self-custody wallet** — Long-term storage

## Risks of Crypto Neobanks

- **Not a bank for crypto** — Most neobanks are not licensed to hold crypto as a custodian
- **Limited coins** — Usually only support BTC, ETH, and a few others
- **Higher fees** — Spread and trading fees are higher than dedicated exchanges
- **Account freezes** — Some neobanks freeze accounts for crypto-related activity
- **No self-custody** — You don't control the private keys

## Verdict
Crypto neobanks are most useful for daily spending, small purchases, and holding small amounts. For significant crypto holdings, use a dedicated exchange for buying and a hardware wallet for storage. Use the neobank as the bridge between crypto and everyday spending.
