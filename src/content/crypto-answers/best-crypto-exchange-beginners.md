**Question from BitcoinTalk:** "Which exchange should I use as a beginner?"

**Short answer:** If you're starting with small amounts, use Coinbase for simplicity or Binance for lower fees. If you're in India, WazirX connects directly to your bank. Here's the full breakdown.

---

Picking your first exchange is confusing. There are hundreds of them, each with different fees, features, and security levels. The good news: for a beginner, only a handful matter.

## What to Look for in an Exchange

Before comparing names, here are the criteria that matter for a beginner:

1. **Easy bank deposits** — Can you deposit from your local bank account?
2. **Low fees** — You don't want to lose 5% to fees on every buy
3. **Good security** — 2FA, cold storage, insurance (if available)
4. **Beginner-friendly** — Clean interface, good mobile app
5. **Your country** — Some exchanges don't serve certain countries

## Detailed Reviews

### Coinbase — Best for Absolute Beginners

Coinbase is the most beginner-friendly exchange. The interface is clean, the mobile app is excellent, and it explains what you're doing at every step.

**Pros:**
- Very easy to use — buy Bitcoin in 3 taps
- Available in most countries
- Insured custody (if Coinbase gets hacked, you're covered)
- Coinbase Earn lets you earn free crypto by learning

**Cons:**
- High fees (0.5% for simple trades, up to 3.5% for credit cards)
- Fewer coins than Binance
- Customer support can be slow

**Verdict:** Start here if you want the easiest experience. Switch to Binance once you learn the basics.

<aside class="callout tip">
💡 <strong>Tip:</strong> Use Coinbase Advanced Trade (free) instead of the regular app — fees drop from 2% to 0.5%.
</aside>

### Binance — Best for Low Fees

Binance has the lowest fees of any major exchange (0.1%). It also has the most coins, advanced trading features, and staking.

**Pros:**
- Lowest fees among major exchanges
- Huge selection of coins
- Advanced features (futures, margin, staking)
- Binance Academy has great learning resources

**Cons:**
- Interface is overwhelming for beginners
- Not available in all countries (banned in UK, Ontario, some US states)
- Regulatory issues in multiple countries

**Verdict:** Best for fees and features once you know what you're doing.

### WazirX — Best for India

WazirX is the most popular exchange in India. It connects directly to UPI and bank transfers, making deposits instant.

**Pros:**
- Direct UPI and bank transfer support
- INR pairs (buy with rupees directly)
- Own wallet app (WazirX Wallet)
- Auto-invest feature for DCA

**Cons:**
- Higher fees on INR deposits and withdrawals
- Fewer coins than Binance
- Regulatory uncertainty in India

**Verdict:** If you're in India, WazirX is the easiest way to buy crypto with rupees.

<aside class="callout warning">
⚠️ <strong>Not your keys, not your coins.</strong> Exchanges hold your crypto. For amounts over $500, move your coins to a personal wallet. Read our guide: <a href="/crypto-basics/which-crypto-wallet-should-you-use/">Which Crypto Wallet Should You Use?</a>
</aside>

### Kraken — Best for Security

Kraken is the oldest major exchange and has never been hacked. It's known for strong security practices and transparency.

**Pros:**
- Excellent security track record
- Proof of reserves (publicly verifiable)
- Low fees (0.16%)
- Staking available

**Cons:**
- Not available in all countries
- Interface can be overwhelming
- Fewer coins than Binance

## What About Decentralized Exchanges?

DEXes like Uniswap and PancakeSwap let you trade directly from your wallet without a middleman. They're more private but harder to use and not recommended for beginners.

Stick with a centralized exchange (CEX) for your first purchases. It's safer, easier, and you can always move your crypto to your own wallet later.

<aside class="callout danger">
🚨 <strong>Never keep large amounts on an exchange.</strong> Exchanges get hacked, shut down, or freeze accounts. Move your crypto to your own wallet once you buy it.
</aside>
