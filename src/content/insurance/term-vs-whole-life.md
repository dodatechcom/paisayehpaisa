The life insurance industry pushes whole life insurance because agents earn 10x more commission. But for 90% of people, term life is the smarter choice.

Here's the math that proves it.

<img src="/images/term-vs-whole-life.svg" alt="Term life vs whole life insurance comparison — costs, benefits, and who needs each" style="width:100%; max-width:760px; height:auto; margin:1.5rem 0; border-radius:8px;" />

## The Quick Comparison

| Factor | Term Life | Whole Life |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Coverage period** | 10, 20, or 30 years | Lifetime |
| **Monthly cost** | $20-40 | $250-400 |
| **Cash value** | None | Yes (1-2% return) |
| **Death benefit** | Guaranteed if you die in term | Guaranteed |
| **Complexity** | Simple | Complex |
| **Agent commission** | 50-70% first year | 50-110% first year |

## The Cost Difference

For a 30-year-old, non-smoker, $500K coverage:

| Type | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost | 30-Year Total |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Term (30-year)** | $25 | $300 | $9,000 |
| **Whole Life** | $350 | $4,200 | $126,000 |
| **Difference** | $325/mo | $3,900/yr | $117,000 |

**That $325/month difference invested in the S&P 500 at 10% = $750,000 after 30 years.**

<aside class="callout info">
📌 <strong>The math is clear:</strong> Buy term life for $25/month and invest the $325/month difference. You'll have $750,000 at the end of 30 years — far more than whole life's cash value.
</aside>

## When Term Life Is Better (90% of People)

| Situation | Why Term |
|-----------|---------|
| **Young family** | Need high coverage at low cost |
| **Mortgage holder** | Cover the mortgage duration |
| **Income earner** | Replace income for dependents |
| **Budget-conscious** | Affordable protection |
| **Temporary need** | Coverage until kids are grown |

## When Whole Life Might Make Sense

| Situation | Why Whole Life |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Estate planning** | Wealth transfer for estates >$12M |
| **Business succession** | Buy-sell agreements |
| **Special needs child** | Lifetime care funding |
| **Maxed out all retirement accounts** | Tax-advantaged cash value |
| **High-net-worth (>$5M)** | Tax diversification |

<aside class="callout warning">
⚠️ <strong>Most whole life sales are wrong.</strong> If an agent says "buy whole life as an investment," they're likely motivated by commission. A $350/month whole life policy pays the agent $2,000-4,000 in commission. A $25/month term policy pays $50-100.
</aside>
