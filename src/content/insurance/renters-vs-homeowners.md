Your landlord's insurance doesn't cover your stuff. If there's a fire, theft, or flood, your laptop, furniture, and clothes are gone — unless you have renters insurance.

Here's what each policy covers and why you need it.

<img src="/images/renters-vs-homeowners.svg" alt="Renters vs homeowners insurance — what each covers, costs, and who needs each" style="width:100%; max-width:760px; height:auto; margin:1.5rem 0; border-radius:8px;" />

## Quick Comparison

| Factor | Renters Insurance | Homeowners Insurance |
|--------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Covers** | Your stuff only | Building + your stuff |
| **Cost** | $15-30/month | $100-200/month |
| **Liability** | $100K | $300K+ |
| **Building coverage** | No | Yes |
| **Required by** | Some landlords | Mortgage lenders |

## What Renters Insurance Covers

| Covered | Not Covered |
|---------|------------|
| Theft of personal property | The building itself |
| Fire damage to belongings | Flooding (separate policy) |
| Water damage (burst pipe) | Earthquakes (separate) |
| Liability (someone hurt in your apt) | Roommate's belongings |
| Additional living expenses | Your car |
| Medical payments to others | Flood damage |

**Personal property limit:** $15,000-$30,000 (increase if needed)

## What Homeowners Insurance Covers

| Covered | Not Covered |
|---------|------------|
| Dwelling (the building) | Flooding (separate policy) |
| Personal property ($50-100K) | Earthquakes (separate) |
| Other structures (garage, shed) | Neglect/poor maintenance |
| Liability ($300K+) | War/nuclear hazard |
| Additional living expenses | Your car |
| Medical payments to others | Foundation issues |

## Real-World Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Apartment Fire

| Without Renters | With Renters |
|----------------|-------------|
| Lost: $20K in belongings | Lost: $20K in belongings |
| Insurance pays: $0 | Insurance pays: $20K |
| Hotel costs: $3,000 out of pocket | Hotel costs: $3,000 paid |
| **Total loss: $23,000** | **Total loss: $0** |

### Scenario 2: Kitchen Fire Destroys Home

| Without Homeowners | With Homeowners |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Building damage: $200K | Building damage: $200K |
| Insurance pays: $0 | Insurance pays: $200K |
| Contents: $50K lost | Contents: $50K paid |
| **Total loss: $250K** | **Total loss: $0** |

## How Much Coverage Do You Need

### Renters

| Item | Coverage Needed |
|------|----------------|
| Electronics | $3,000-5,000 |
| Furniture | $3,000-5,000 |
| Clothing | $2,000-5,000 |
| Jewelry | $1,000-2,000 |
| Other items | $2,000-5,000 |
| **Total recommended** | **$20,000-25,000** |

### Homeowners

| Item | Coverage Needed |
|------|----------------|
| Dwelling | Replacement cost of home |
| Personal property | 50-70% of dwelling coverage |
| Liability | $300K minimum |
| Deductible | $1,000-2,500 |

## Cost Comparison

| Policy Type | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost |
|------------|-------------|-------------|
| Renters (basic) | $15 | $180 |
| Renters (good coverage) | $25 | $300 |
| Homeowners (basic) | $100 | $1,200 |
| Homeowners (good coverage) | $150 | $1,800 |

<aside class="callout info">
📌 <strong>Bundle and save:</strong> Most insurers offer 10-20% discounts when you bundle renters/homeowners with auto insurance. That's $50-200/year in savings.
</aside>

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|------------|
| **Skipping renters insurance** | $20K+ loss if theft/fire |
| **Underinsuring home** | Can't rebuild after disaster |
| **Not updating policy** | Coverage gaps |
| **Ignoring flood insurance** | Not covered by standard policy |
| **Choosing wrong deductible** | Can't afford $2,500 deductible |

## Summary

| Key Point | Takeaway |
|-----------|----------|
| **Renters insurance** | $15-30/month, protects your stuff |
| **Homeowners insurance** | $100-200/month, required by lenders |
| **Both exclude** | Flooding and earthquakes |
| **Bundle** | Auto + home/renters = discount |
| **Don't skip** | One fire can cost $20K+ |

</aside>
