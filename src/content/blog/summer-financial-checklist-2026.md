Summer is the perfect time to get your finances in order before the year races away. Use this 10-point checklist to make sure you're not leaving money on the table.

## 1. Check Your Savings Rates

Interest rates shift frequently. The best easy-access account today may not be the best next month.

| Action | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **Compare current rates** | Top easy-access accounts pay 4.5%+ |
| **Check notice accounts** | 30-90 day notice accounts pay more |
| **Move money to the best account** | Even 0.5% extra on £10,000 = £50/year |

Use comparison sites like Moneyfacts or Save the Best to find the top rates. Don't let your savings sit in a 0.1% account when you could earn 4.5%.

## 2. Review Insurance Before Renewal

Auto-renewing insurance is one of the biggest wastes of money in the UK. Insurers rely on inertia.

| Insurance Type | Potential Saving |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Car insurance** | £200-£400 by switching |
| **Home insurance** | £100-£200 by switching |
| **Travel insurance** | £50-£100 by switching |

Start comparing 3-4 weeks before your renewal date. Use comparison sites, then check the insurer's direct quote — it's often cheaper.

## 3. Use Your ISA Allowance

The tax year ends in April, but planning ahead means you won't scramble at the last minute.

| ISA Type | 2025/26 Allowance | Key Benefit |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Cash ISA** | £20,000 | Tax-free interest |
| **Stocks & Shares ISA** | £20,000 | Tax-free gains |
| **Lifetime ISA** | £4,000 | 25% government bonus |
| **Innovative Finance ISA** | £20,000 | Tax-free peer-to-peer interest |

If you haven't used your allowance yet, prioritise a Lifetime ISA if you're under 40 and saving for a home or retirement. The 25% bonus is free money.

## 4. Check Pension Contributions

Most people under-contribute to their pension. A small increase now makes a huge difference thanks to compound growth.

| Contribution Increase | Extra at Retirement (age 25-65) |
|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| **£50/month extra** | ~£96,000 more |
| **£100/month extra** | ~£192,000 more |
| **£200/month extra** | ~£384,000 more |

Check if your employer matches contributions above the minimum. Many will match up to 5-6% — that's an instant 100% return on your money.

## 5. Review Your Credit Report

Your credit report affects your ability to get mortgages, credit cards, and even phone contracts. Check it for free.

| Service | What You Get |
|---------|-------------|
| **ClearScore** | Experian report, free forever |
| **Credit Karma** | TransUnion report, free forever |
| **MoneySavingExpert Credit Club** | Experian report, free |

Look for errors, outdated information, or fraudulent accounts. Fixing a mistake could improve your score by 50-100 points.

## 6. Check Your Tax Code

A wrong tax code means you could be overpaying HMRC. The average refund is over £1,000.

| Tax Code | What It Means |
|----------|--------------|
| **1257L** | Standard — you earn £12,570 tax-free |
| **BR** | All income taxed at 20% — usually wrong |
| **D0** | All income taxed at 40% — check if correct |
| **NT** | No tax — verify this applies to you |

Check your tax code on your payslip or via your HMRC online account. If it looks wrong, call HMRC to get it corrected.

## 7. Cancel Unused Subscriptions

The average UK household wastes £60/month on subscriptions they don't use. That's £720/year going down the drain.

| Common Wasted Subscriptions | Average Monthly Cost |
|----------------------------|---------------------|
| **Streaming services** | £10-£15 each |
| **Gym memberships** | £30-£50 |
| **Magazine/newspaper apps** | £5-£15 |
| **Software you don't use** | £5-£20 |

Go through your bank statements and cancel anything you haven't used in the last month. Use a subscription tracker app to stay on top of renewals.

## 8. Compare Broadband and Energy Deals

Loyalty doesn't pay when it comes to broadband and energy. Switching regularly saves hundreds.

| Deal Type | Potential Annual Saving |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Broadband** | £100-£200 |
| **Mobile phone** | £60-£120 |
| **Energy** | £200-£400 |

For broadband, check if your contract has ended — you'll be on out-of-contract rates which can be double the price. Use Uswitch or Compare the Market to find the best deal.

## 9. Update Your Will and Lasting Power of Attorney

Life changes — marriages, divorces, births, deaths, property purchases — all affect your will. If it doesn't reflect your current situation, your wishes may not be followed.

| Document | Why Update It |
|----------|--------------|
| **Will** | Ensure assets go where you want |
| **LPA (Property & Financial)** | Someone can manage your finances if you can't |
| **LPA (Health & Welfare)** | Someone can make medical decisions for you |

It costs £82 per LPA to register with the Office of the Public Guardian. Without one, your family may face months of legal complications.

## 10. Set Up an Emergency Fund

An emergency fund protects you from unexpected costs — job loss, car repairs, medical bills. Without one, you'll rely on credit cards and debt.

| Fund Size | Coverage |
|-----------|----------|
| **£1,000** | Starter emergency fund |
| **3 months expenses** | Basic safety net |
| **6 months expenses** | Full protection |

If your monthly expenses are £2,000, aim for £6,000-£12,000 in an easy-access savings account. Start with £1,000, then build from there.

<aside class="callout tip">
💡 <strong>Start with 2-3 items from this checklist.</strong> You don't have to do everything at once. Tackle one or two per weekend and you'll be in great shape by September.
</aside>
