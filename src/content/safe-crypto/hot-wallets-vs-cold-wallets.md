Every crypto holder needs a wallet. The first decision: hot or cold?

**Hot wallet** = connected to the internet (app, browser extension). Convenient but vulnerable to hacks.
**Cold wallet** = offline device (hardware wallet). Extremely secure but less convenient.

Most people need both. Here's how to decide.

<aside class="callout danger">
🚨 <strong>Keeping crypto on an exchange is NOT the same as a wallet.</strong> If an exchange collapses (FTX, Celsius, BlockFi), your funds may be lost. A wallet gives you control. Read: <a href="/crypto-basics/which-crypto-wallet-should-you-use/">Which Crypto Wallet Should You Use?</a>
</aside>

## Hot Wallets

Hot wallets are software programs connected to the internet. They include mobile apps, browser extensions, and desktop programs.

**Examples:** MetaMask, Trust Wallet, Exodus, Phantom, Coinbase Wallet

### Pros
- **Free** — No purchase required
- **Fast** — Send and receive in seconds
- **Convenient** — Access your crypto anywhere, anytime
- **DeFi ready** — Connect to dApps, swap tokens, stake
- **Good for small amounts** — Keep spending money accessible

### Cons
- **Internet-connected** — Vulnerable to malware, phishing, and keyloggers
- **Private key is on your device** — If your phone/computer is compromised, your crypto is at risk
- **Phishing target** — Fake websites and apps can steal your seed phrase
- **Not for large amounts** — Not safe for significant savings

## Cold Wallets

Cold wallets (hardware wallets) are physical devices that store your private keys offline. They never connect to the internet directly.

**Examples:** Ledger Nano S/X, Trezor Model One/T, Coldcard, Keystone

### Pros
- **Extremely secure** — Private keys never touch the internet
- **Hack-proof** — Even if your computer has malware, your crypto is safe
- **Transaction confirmation** — You physically confirm transactions on the device
- **Long-term storage** — Ideal for holding for years

### Cons
- **Costs money** — $50-200 for the device
- **Less convenient** — Need the device to send crypto
- **Can be lost or damaged** — Lose the device without your seed phrase backup = lose your crypto
- **Not ideal for frequent trading** — Each transaction requires connecting the device

## Comparison Table

| Feature | Hot Wallet | Cold Wallet |
|---|---|---|
| Security | Low-Medium | Very High |
| Cost | Free | $50-200 |
| Convenience | Very high | Low-Medium |
| Best for | Daily spending, small amounts | Long-term holding, large amounts |
| Risk | Malware, phishing, hacks | Physical loss, damage |
| Setup time | 2 minutes | 15-30 minutes |

## Which Amounts Should Go Where?

| Amount | Recommended Wallet |
|---|---|
| Up to $500 | Hot wallet (Trust Wallet, MetaMask) |
| $500 - $5,000 | Hot wallet + 2FA |
| $5,000 - $50,000 | Cold wallet (Ledger, Trezor) |
| $50,000+ | Cold wallet + multisig |

<aside class="callout tip">
💡 <strong>Use both:</strong> Keep your daily spending crypto (small amounts) in a hot wallet. Store the rest in a cold wallet. Most hardware wallets have companion apps (Ledger Live) that let you manage both from one interface.
</aside>

## Software Wallets vs Hardware Wallets

There's also a middle ground: software wallets on dedicated devices.

**Software wallet on a clean device:** Some people install a wallet app on an old phone or laptop that stays offline. This gives hardware-level security without buying a device.

**Risk:** If the device ever connects to the internet, security is compromised.

## Wallet Safety Checklist

Regardless of which wallet you choose:

- [ ] Write down your seed phrase on paper — NOT in a file, screenshot, or cloud
- [ ] Store the seed phrase in a fireproof safe or safety deposit box
- [ ] Never enter your seed phrase into any website or app
- [ ] Test your recovery process with a small amount first
- [ ] Use a strong password for your wallet app
- [ ] Enable biometric lock (fingerprint/Face ID) on mobile wallets

<aside class="callout danger">
🚨 <strong>Your seed phrase is your crypto.</strong> Anyone with your seed phrase can steal your funds. Never type it online, never screenshot it, never share it with anyone including "support staff." No legitimate company will ever ask for your seed phrase.
</aside>

## Verdict

Use a **hot wallet** for small amounts you spend regularly (up to $500). Use a **cold wallet** for everything else.

If you have more than $1,000 in crypto, buy a hardware wallet. A $100 Ledger protects thousands or tens of thousands. It's the best investment you'll make in crypto security.

*This question appears multiple times daily on BitcoinTalk. The universal answer: cold storage for long-term holdings, hot wallets for spending money.*
