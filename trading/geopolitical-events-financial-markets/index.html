How Geopolitical Events Affect Financial Markets (Forex & Crypto)

June 16, 2026
🏷️ geopolitics 🏷️ macro 🏷️ news-trading 🏷️ strategy

“Did anyone invest in crypto during the US-Iran war?”

This question appeared on Myfxbook’s crypto forum during the 2026 Middle East tensions. The answer reveals something important: geopolitical events affect all markets — forex, crypto, stocks — but in different ways.

Understanding how is the difference between panic and opportunity.

Why Geopolitics Moves Markets

Geopolitical events change the fundamental conditions that drive asset prices. They affect:

  • Interest rate expectations — Wars cause inflation, central banks respond
  • Risk sentiment — Investors flee to safe havens or chase risk
  • Supply chains — Sanctions disrupt trade, affecting currencies and commodities
  • Capital flows — Money moves between countries and asset classes

Traders who understand these connections can anticipate moves before they happen. Those who don’t get caught on the wrong side.

💡 Geopolitical events create volatility, not direction. The market's reaction often matters more than the event itself. A war in one region might strengthen or weaken a currency depending on how global markets interpret it.

How Geopolitics Affects Forex

Forex is the most geopolitically sensitive market because currencies are directly tied to countries.

Key geopolitical drivers in forex:

Central bank decisions: When a central bank raises rates, its currency strengthens. When it cuts, its currency weakens. Rate decisions are influenced by inflation, which is influenced by geopolitics. A war that raises oil prices causes inflation, which forces rate hikes, which strengthens that country’s currency.

Elections: Currency markets react to election outcomes. A pro-business government generally strengthens the currency. A populist government that threatens trade deals weakens it. Example: the Indian rupee often weakens during election uncertainty and stabilizes after clear results.

Sanctions and trade wars: Sanctions on a country weaken its currency. The Russian ruble dropped sharply after 2022 sanctions. Trade wars between the US and China affected USD/CNY and emerging market currencies.

Military conflicts: Currency reactions are complex. The US dollar often strengthens during global conflicts because it is the world’s reserve currency — investors buy USD as a safe haven. But the currency of a conflict zone (e.g., Ukrainian hryvnia during the Russia-Ukraine war) collapses.

Safe haven currencies:

  • USD, JPY, CHF — tend to strengthen during global uncertainty
  • Emerging market currencies — tend to weaken during global uncertainty
⚠️ News trading is risky for beginners. During major geopolitical events, spreads widen dramatically, slippage increases, and price can gap through stop losses. Professional traders often step aside and wait for clarity.

How Geopolitics Affects Crypto

Crypto reacts to geopolitics differently from forex.

Bitcoin as digital gold: During some geopolitical crises, Bitcoin has acted as a safe haven. During the 2023 US banking crisis, Bitcoin rallied as people sought an alternative to the traditional banking system. During the 2025 trade war escalation, Bitcoin also rose as a hedge against currency debasement.

Bitcoin as risk asset: But during other crises, Bitcoin has dropped alongside stocks. When Russia invaded Ukraine in 2022, Bitcoin fell sharply — it behaved like a risk asset, not a safe haven. This dual nature confuses beginners.

The pattern:

  • Immediate shock — Bitcoin drops (liquidity crunch, panic selling)
  • Recovery phase — Bitcoin recovers as people seek alternatives to traditional systems
  • Long-term — Depends on whether the crisis strengthens or weakens the case for decentralized money

Regulatory impact: Geopolitical events often trigger regulatory responses. After the FTX collapse, regulators worldwide cracked down on crypto exchanges. After the US approved Bitcoin ETFs, other countries followed. Regulation follows geopolitics.

Capital controls: During currency crises (Venezuela, Nigeria, Lebanon), people turn to Bitcoin as a way to bypass capital controls. This creates local demand that can drive Bitcoin prices in those regions higher than global prices.

What to Watch as a Trader

Monitor these indicators daily:

Central bank calendars — Know when the Fed, RBI, ECB, and BOJ meet Election calendars — Major elections in the US, India, UK, and EU Conflict zones — Ukraine-Russia, Middle East, Taiwan strait, Korea Commodity prices — Oil, gold, and grain prices signal geopolitical stress Sanctions announcements — New sanctions on major economies

How to trade around geopolitical events:

  1. Reduce position size — Volatility means wider stops and more slippage
  2. Widen your stops — Normal technical levels will get blown through
  3. Use limit orders — Market orders during news events have severe slippage
  4. Wait for clarity — The first reaction is often wrong; the second move is the real move
  5. Watch correlated markets — If oil is spiking and gold is rallying, expect forex pairs to follow
💡 The trend is your friend during geopolitics. If a currency weakens after an election, do not try to catch the bottom. The trend can persist for weeks or months. Trade with it, not against it.

Verdict

Geopolitical events are unpredictable, but their market effects follow patterns. Safe haven currencies (USD, JPY, CHF) strengthen during crises. Bitcoin is unpredictable in the short term but benefits from long-term currency debasement narratives. Emerging market currencies weaken.

The best strategy: stay informed, reduce risk during uncertainty, and wait for clear trends before entering. Trying to predict geopolitical outcomes is a losing game. Reacting to them with discipline is not.
